Annotated process · 15 steps

Building a Help Center That Deflects

01

Build product knowledge

Verified feature list, UI labels, plan tiers, capability descriptions. Every claim in every article traces back to this file. Write nothing without it.

02

Audit existing docs

Score on accuracy, customer-centricity, structure, completeness. Prioritize rewrites by ticket volume and page views. Output: P0/P1/P2 list before a single word is written.

03

Map content to customer journey

Setup → core concept → core action → power feature → edge case. Five questions per feature: what is it, how do I do it, how do I undo it, who can do it, what happens after.

04

Design taxonomy

Hybrid task-first model. Verb-first top-level categories ("Make and receive calls"). Feature-named articles inside. 3 levels max. 5–7 top-level categories. Miller's number.

05

Apply article templates

Per type: how-to, onboarding narrative, conceptual, integration, troubleshooting, reference. Every template has title, one-line outcome, prerequisite block, body, related articles.

06

Progressive disclosure

One-line TL;DR first. Step-by-step second. Edge cases and admin-only options in expandable sections. Write for the person who reads the first sentence and then acts.
