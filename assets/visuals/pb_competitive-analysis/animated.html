Phase 1 · Intelligence gathering

Build the evidence base

01

Scope and tier rivals

Tier by CRM revenue impact. ~5 rivals cover 80% of deals. Add the status quo.

02

Centralize existing intel

Pull CRM notes, CS calls, and rep memory before any external research.

03

Research Tier-1 rivals

Trials + 2–3-star reviews + job postings. Posts reveal strategy 6 months early.

04

Run win/loss interviews

CRM tells you who. Interviews tell you why. Separate status-quo from competitive losses.

05

Build competitor profiles

One wrong claim breaks seller trust permanently. Concede real strengths honestly.

Sources: April Dunford · Crayon · Gartner a builder's codex
Phase 2 · Analysis and enablement

Turn intel into weapons

06

Build the differentiation matrix

6–8 dimensions sourced from win/loss interviews. Each needs a proof point.

07

Run a SWOT per Tier-1 rival

SW = internal. OT = market movements. Specific enough to act on.

08

Build battlecards

Why We Win · Strengths + responses · Landmines. One to two pages, validated.

09

Write strategic recommendations

One recommendation per audience: sales, product, marketing. Thirty-day horizon.

10

Enable, distribute, refresh

Battlecards in CRM at deal level. Weekly objection log. Refresh on event.

Sources: April Dunford · Crayon · Gartner a builder's codex
