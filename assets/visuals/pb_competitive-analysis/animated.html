Annotated pipeline · 10 steps across 2 phases

The Competitive Intel System

Phase 1 · Intelligence gathering

01

Scope and tier rivals

Define landscape boundaries. Tier by CRM revenue impact: Tier 1 = ~5 players in ~80% of competitive deals. Full profile + battlecard for each. Include the status quo as an implicit competitor.

02

Centralize existing intel

Pull from CRM deal notes, sales decks, CS call summaries, prior reports. Everything in one place before a single new search runs. Reps know months before dashboards do.

03

Research Tier-1 rivals

Primary: sign up for trials, read pricing, review platforms (2- and 3-star reviews specifically). Secondary: job postings, analyst reports, BuiltWith. Job posts reveal product strategy six months early.

04

Run win/loss interviews

CRM disposition codes tell you who. Prospect interviews (5-10 won, 5-10 lost) tell you why. Separate status-quo losses from competitive losses. The fix is different for each.

05

Build competitor profiles

Per Tier-1 rival: positioning, product strengths, pricing model, buyer sentiment from reviews. One wrong claim breaks seller trust permanently. Acknowledge competitor strengths honestly.

Phase 2 · Analysis and enablement

06

Build the differentiation matrix

6-8 dimensions only. Dimensions come from win/loss interviews, not your product team's feature list. Each dimension needs a proof point. Test with external audiences before publishing.

07

Run a SWOT per Tier-1 rival

Strengths and weaknesses are internal. Opportunities and threats are market movements. Keep entries specific enough to act on. "Integration is a weakness" is not actionable. "Integration vs. Competitor X in RevOps deals" is.

08

Build battlecards

Three sections: Why We Win (with deal references), Competitor Strengths with copy-paste responses, Landmines (questions reps can plant). One to two scannable pages. Validated by top performers before shipping.

09

Write strategic recommendations

One recommendation per audience: sales (which competitors to prioritize), product (capability gaps in loss reasons), marketing (positioning adjustments). Specific enough to act on in 30 days.

10

Enable, distribute, refresh

Embed battlecards in CRM at the opportunity level. Brief the team live with roleplay. Weekly competitive objection log: reps paste one objection per week, you review Friday. Battlecard refreshes on event, not just quarterly.
