Kieran Klaassen. General Manager of Cora, Every's AI email product, and the creator of the Compound Engineering methodology. Klaassen came to software from a background in film composition before becoming a startup CTO, and developed Compound Engineering from the ground up while building Cora with AI coding agents. He co-authored the original "Compound Engineering: How Every Codes With Agents" essay with Dan Shipper in December 2025, then expanded it to an eight-step framework in May 2026 to formalize the human roles that remain after agents take over execution.

## Operating themes
- **Human-bookend principle** The human engineer's irreplaceable contribution sits at the start (deciding what to build) and the end (judging whether the result is right); agents execute everything between.
- **Compounding artifacts** Each completed feature should produce documented learnings that make the next feature cheaper and faster to build.
- **Specification discipline** Rigorous ideation and planning before handing work to an agent determines result quality more than any mid-cycle intervention.
