Adam Mosseri is the Head of Instagram at Meta, where he oversees the platform's product direction, algorithm, and creator ecosystem. He joined Facebook in 2008 and has led Instagram since 2018. His public commentary covers platform design, creator economics, and how social platforms respond to structural changes in content production. He speaks regularly on how platforms should navigate the shift toward AI-generated content while preserving creator trust and user engagement.

## Operating themes
- **AI content as a labeling problem, not a blocking problem.** Mosseri consistently argues that platforms should help users distinguish AI from human content through labeling and creator identity signals, not through restrictions on creation.
- **Authenticity scarcity in high-volume content environments.** His thesis is that as AI-generated content becomes abundant, the relative scarcity of genuine human perspective drives up the value of authentic creators on platform.
- **Algorithm transparency.** He has argued that the Instagram algorithm is only recently catching up to what users assumed it understood years ago, and that clearer explanations of discovery mechanics benefit both creators and users.
