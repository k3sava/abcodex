Dan Shipper. CEO and co-founder of Every, an AI-native media and software company that grew from four employees to thirty between the GPT-3 era and 2026 while automating everything it could automate. Every produces writing on AI, productivity, and business, and builds AI software tools including Lex, the AI-augmented writing editor, and Spiral, an AI research assistant. Shipper writes about what actually happens when a company automates its own work end to end, and what remains after automation: the framing, the taste, the contact with present reality that no model can substitute. His "After Automation" essay (May 2026) is a primary-source account of running an AI-native company at scale and noticing the paradox that automation creates more human work, not less.

## Operating themes
- **Framers vs. executors** AI excels at execution within a defined frame; the premium moves to framers who maintain contact with current reality and decide what problems matter now.
- **Automation reveals differentiation gaps** Commoditizing expert output with AI exposes companies whose value was delivery speed; buyers then select on what's genuinely different.
- **Building in public at the AI frontier** Shipper documents what it actually looks like to run an AI-native team, including the tradeoffs, paradoxes, and productivity patterns no benchmark captures.
