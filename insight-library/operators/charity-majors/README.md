Charity Majors. Co-founder and CTO of Honeycomb, the observability platform built around high-cardinality event data and rapid debugging of distributed systems. Majors pioneered the modern observability discipline, drawing on engineering leadership roles at Parse (acquired by Facebook) and Linden Lab. She co-authored Observability Engineering and Database Reliability Engineering (O'Reilly). Her writing, published at charity.wtf and her Substack, covers engineering leadership, organizational health, and the practical consequences of AI adoption in software teams. Her work on feedback loops in engineering organizations is a foundational reference for teams adopting agentic AI tooling.

## Operating themes
- **AI as organizational amplifier** AI deployment reveals and accelerates existing organizational strengths and dysfunctions; teams with tight feedback loops improve, teams without them deteriorate faster.
- **Feedback loops as the core infrastructure of engineering** Fast, precise, and relevant feedback enables self-awareness in individuals and self-governance in teams; without them, AI autonomy compounds errors invisibly.
- **The asymmetry of wins and costs** In AI adoption, wins are public and celebrated; costs accumulate privately in on-call rotations, cleanup work, and eroded trust. Closing that gap is the central leadership challenge.
