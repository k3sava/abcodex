Charity Majors. Co-founder and CTO of Honeycomb.io, the observability platform built for distributed systems. Majors writes about engineering leadership, database internals, on-call culture, and the organizational realities of AI adoption. She observes AI adoption from a position that spans both sides: Honeycomb customers shipping faster with AI tools and the same customers opening incidents because no one on the team understands the code that shipped. That vantage point gives her arguments about AI in engineering organizations an evidence base most analysts lack.

## Operating themes
- **AI as organizational amplifier** AI accelerates strengths and dysfunctions equally; the organizational design determines which dominates.
- **Feedback loop gaps in adoption** Wins from AI-assisted development are public; costs accumulate in private channels (on-call, post-mortems). The structural absence of a shared feedback loop is the primary failure mode of AI adoption programs.
- **Engineering discipline as the corrective** Reframing AI disagreements as engineering problems ("What would it take to feel comfortable shipping code you haven't read?") moves conversations from philosophy to tractable questions.
