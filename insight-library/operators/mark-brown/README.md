Mark Brown. Game designer, video essayist, and creator of Game Maker's Toolkit, a long-running series examining game design through specific, concrete examples. Brown is currently developing his own roguelike game and documenting the process publicly, producing detailed technical essays on the architecture decisions he makes along the way. His work sits at the intersection of game design theory and practical implementation, with particular attention to how code structure shapes design possibility. No em dashes, no marketing abstraction: he writes about what he actually built and why it worked or failed.

## Operating themes
- **Concrete architecture over abstract principle** Brown traces design claims back to specific code decisions (class hierarchies, trigger functions, state machines) rather than general guidance.
- **Public development as design documentation** He treats his roguelike project as a live case study, publishing the reasoning behind each implementation choice as it happens.
- **Scaling as the test of a design decision** His analysis often tracks whether a pattern holds up at 10x the initial complexity, not just at the point it was first adopted.
