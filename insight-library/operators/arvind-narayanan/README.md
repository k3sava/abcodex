Arvind Narayanan is a computer science professor at Princeton whose research spans security, privacy, and the social implications of AI. His newsletter and book AI Snake Oil, co-authored with Sayash Kapoor, applies rigorous empirical scrutiny to claims about AI capabilities, separating documented effects from vendor hype. He writes at the intersection of technical AI research and labor economics, grounding claims about job displacement in WARN Act filings, Federal Reserve employment data, and controlled studies rather than projections.

## Operating themes
- **Evidence-first AI claims** Applies the same standards to AI hype that he applies to any empirical claim: burden of proof, effect size, and alternative explanations before accepting a narrative.
- **Structural labor analysis** Examines which layers of work AI actually automates versus which layers remain human, using the decide-execute-deliver sandwich as an organizing framework.
- **AI policy and safety** Writes on the relationship between AI governance, infrastructure readiness, and capability deployment pacing.
