Benedict Evans. Independent technology analyst whose annual strategy decks and essays have shaped how investors, founders, and operators think about platform transitions: mobile, media, commerce, and now AI. Evans spent years as a general partner at Andreessen Horowitz before returning to independent analysis. His work focuses on the structural dynamics of technology markets, tracing where capital flows, where value accumulates, and where margin gets compressed across the stack.

## Operating themes
- **Value migration up the stack** Evans traces how infrastructure investments compound at lower layers while value and margin concentrate at higher-layer products and platforms.
- **Historical analogy as forecast** He applies telecom, semiconductor, and media precedents to current AI market structure, identifying where the analogy holds and where the AI case diverges.
- **Supply and demand over narrative** His analysis grounds in capital flows, cost structure, and efficiency curves rather than in capability claims or competitive rhetoric.
