Anthropic launched Claude for Small Business on May 14 — a set of pre-built connectors that put Claude inside QuickBooks, PayPal, HubSpot, Canva, DocuSign, Google Workspace, and Microsoft 365. No custom dev required. Pick the tools you already use, connect them, and Claude runs workflows inside them. Anthropic is pairing this with a free city-by-city hands-on workshop roadshow across US cities. Same day, Anthropic announced a $200 million partnership with the Gates Foundation — Claude credits, grant funding, and technical support for programs in global health, education, and economic mobility over four years. And PwC deepened its alliance with a joint Center of Excellence and a commitment to train 30,000 PwC professionals on Claude. Three announcements in one day, all pointing at the same thesis: Claude as embedded infrastructure, not a standalone tool.

For consulting: the SMB connector story is now a concrete pitch. Clients who've been waiting for AI that doesn't need an engineering team to deploy have their answer. For ab-codex: this is a textbook "infrastructure embedding" launch — worth capturing as an insight on how AI companies are marketing to non-technical buyers by meeting them inside familiar tools.
