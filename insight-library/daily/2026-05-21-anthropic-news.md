On May 19, Anthropic and KPMG announced a global alliance. All 276,000+ KPMG employees get Claude access through KPMG's Digital Gateway platform. The deal goes further than access: KPMG Blaze, a new product, embeds Claude Code specifically to help clients cut IT development lifecycles. KPMG is also named a preferred partner for Anthropic's private equity clients, meaning PE portfolio companies get a direct pipeline to KPMG-delivered Claude builds. This lands right after Anthropic's PwC alliance, making it two of the Big Four in sequence. On May 14, Anthropic also announced a separate $200M commitment with the Gates Foundation across global health, life sciences, and education over four years.

The pattern here is vertical concentration. Anthropic is not waiting for enterprises to figure out AI adoption on their own. It is building a delivery layer through the firms those enterprises already trust for transformation work. For consulting: this narrows the window for independent AI consultants who compete on access or general AI knowledge. The differentiation that survives is narrow-domain expertise and direct client relationships that KPMG cannot replicate at scale. For ab-codex: Anthropic's vertical-by-vertical expansion (legal, finance, now professional services) is worth tracking as a pattern. Each entry is a new set of use cases to document.
