John Cutler's TBM 422 names a tension most product and ops leaders are feeling but not articulating: the gap between exception-handling (putting out fires), presence (staying in context to give good guidance), and delegation (trusting people and systems to run without you). His observation is that cumulative pressure from multiple directions is collapsing the space between these modes — leaders default to firefighting because presence and delegation require slack they don't have. The fatigue is real, and it is eroding the quality of decisions that get made above the line.

For consulting: this is precise scaffolding for PMM and product leader coaching conversations. When clients describe feeling overwhelmed, Cutler's three-mode model names the mechanism — that is far more useful than generic advice about prioritization. For career-ops: Kesava is running substrate, ab-codex, consulting, and several side projects simultaneously. The delegation gap Cutler describes is a direct diagnostic for where attention is leaking.
