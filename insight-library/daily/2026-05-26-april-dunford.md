April Dunford's May 21 post makes a clean argument: AI changes your product, your competitors, and your market all at once. When all three shift, positioning becomes urgent. The trap she flags is substituting investor and peer narratives for actual customer evidence. Companies hear the same AI hype at conferences, update their positioning based on what other companies are saying, and end up with messaging nobody recognizes themselves in.

For consulting and ab-codex: this is directly the problem ab-codex is designed to counteract. Operator-attributed insights give people a check against the investor narrative loop. For Kesava's consulting work, this is a sharp framing device for a positioning audit offer: "When did you last validate your positioning against buyers rather than peers?"
