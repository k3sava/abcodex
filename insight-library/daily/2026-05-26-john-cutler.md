Cutler's TBM 423 focuses on a deceptively simple question: what is a team? His observation is that it is easy to describe how an organization actually works but hard to say it out loud, because the gap between org chart and lived reality is embarrassing and politically dangerous. AI pressure compounds this, because agents can be assigned to tasks that previously required a team boundary, collapsing distinctions that took years to formalize.

For consulting: this is a diagnostic framing for client conversations. "How does work actually move here" is a better opening question than "what does your org chart look like." For career-ops: it is a useful lens for Kesava's own positioning as a fractional operator; the value is in operating across the gap between official structure and actual work, which AI cannot yet navigate politically.
