Rand Fishkin buries Google's 25-year-old advice. "Make great content" worked when content was scarce and Google rewarded depth. It fails when AI produces great-looking content at zero marginal cost. The new rule: build something AI cannot replicate. Not a better blog post. An inimitable product, community, dataset, or network -- something with provenance and proof attached. He also rewires the funnel: influence and impressions across the platforms where attention lives are now top-of-funnel. Branded search volume is middle. Traffic to your own site is a lagging indicator, not a leading one. For ab-codex: operator-attributed insights are the inimitable product. No LLM can replicate "I ran this play at HubSpot in Q3 and here is what broke." The library's entire design is the answer to Fishkin's question. For career-ops and consulting: Kesava's named track record and the companies he can cite are the moat. The goal is not to produce more content -- it is to make the content that already exists undeniably his.
