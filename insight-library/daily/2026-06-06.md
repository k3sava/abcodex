# What landed today, 2026-06-06

Three new cards, one new operator. No patterns: the two operators below share a theme but two voices do not clear the convergence threshold. One thread ran through all three cards: AI is now executing the middle of creative and engineering work. The human's role has migrated to the edges, where judgment and taste live.

## Theme 1, AI now curates what human audiences read

Ethan Mollick published "Co-Existence and the End of Co-Intelligence" on June 4, arguing that the relationship between creators and audiences has inverted. AI recommendation systems now evaluate content before humans discover it. Mollick states this plainly: "I expect that, increasingly, AIs will read my work and choose whether to recommend what I write to their human users."

`ins_ai-as-content-curator` captures the structural shift: the traditional direct path from creator to browsing human audience is now mediated by AI selection criteria. Content that does not pass AI evaluation may not reach human audiences at all, regardless of its quality. The mechanism is distribution-layer capture: as AI agents absorb the discovery function, they apply their own evaluation criteria for corroboration, factual density, and source authority before surfacing results to users.

`ins_write-every-draft-yourself-ai-era` captures the creator's tactical response. Mollick describes the workflow he used for Co-Existence: "I wrote every chapter draft myself" before passing each chapter to a council of AI models for feedback and fact-checking. The first draft is where authentic perspective is established. AI tools can refine, check, and unblock, but they cannot inject the reasoning that makes a work distinguishable. In a world where AI systems evaluate content for recommendation, that distinctiveness is what earns the citation.

- Ethan Mollick, `ins_ai-as-content-curator`. AI recommendation systems now act as the first filter between creators and audiences, inverting the traditional author-to-reader path.
- Ethan Mollick, `ins_write-every-draft-yourself-ai-era`. Writing every first draft yourself before using AI for feedback and fact-checking preserves the authentic perspective that AI curation systems select for.

## Theme 2, Human engineers own the beginning and the end

Kieran Klaassen, General Manager of Cora at Every, published an update to his Compound Engineering methodology on May 29, expanding the framework from four steps to eight. The expansion formalized what the practice had made visible: agents can reliably handle implementation, testing, and pull request preparation, but two phases remain irreducibly human.

`ins_human-bookend-ai-engineering` captures the model. Klaassen describes it as a sandwich: "AI is the stuff in the middle. Humans are the bread on either end." The new steps at the front, Ideate and Brainstorm, formalize the work of deciding which features have genuine product value. The new step at the back, Polish, formalizes the judgment that the result actually feels right beyond technical correctness. The implication is that engineering teams should shift their time upstream and downstream, with execution effort shrinking toward zero as agent quality improves.

The parallel with Theme 1 is not forced: both Mollick on content creation and Klaassen on software development arrived independently at the same structure. AI executes the middle. Human judgment at the edges is what makes the result worth shipping.

- Kieran Klaassen, `ins_human-bookend-ai-engineering`. In AI-native engineering, human contribution concentrates at ideation (deciding what to build) and polish (judging whether it feels right); agents execute everything between.
