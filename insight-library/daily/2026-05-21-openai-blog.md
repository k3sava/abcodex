On May 15, OpenAI launched a $4B+ consulting and deployment venture, backed by TPG, Bain Capital, Brookfield, McKinsey, Capgemini, and others. It will place Forward Deployed Engineers inside client organizations and acquired a company called Tomoro to accelerate the delivery side. The same day, OpenAI launched ChatGPT personal finance tools for Pro subscribers, connecting to 12,000+ financial institutions including Schwab, Fidelity, and Chase. Two different product bets in one announcement: one targets Fortune 500 deployment complexity, the other targets consumer financial trust.

The deployment company is the bigger structural move. OpenAI is now directly competing with the partners backing it. McKinsey and Capgemini are both investors and competitors if their clients call OpenAI instead of them. That tension will show up in who lands the next round of large enterprise contracts. For consulting: this compresses the window for any practice that positions itself as the "AI translation layer" between OpenAI and enterprise clients. The lab itself is now doing that work. The durable position is either very deep domain expertise or a client relationship tight enough that the client calls you first regardless.
