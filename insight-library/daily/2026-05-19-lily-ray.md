Lily Ray posted a warning — backed by a podcast episode with Ross Hudgens of Siege Media — that several popular GEO tactics are being treated as spam signals by both Google and Microsoft. Specifically: self-promotional comparison pages ("X vs. Y, and we're better"), AI-optimized listicles targeting AI citation slots, and prompt injection via "summarize with AI" buttons embedded in content. Her argument is that these work briefly because they pattern-match to what AI systems reward, then get penalized once the engines adjust. She outlines what she considers durable approaches instead: genuine first-person experience, forum-style specificity, and content that earns authority rather than manufactures it.
