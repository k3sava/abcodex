April Dunford identifies three conditions that force a positioning shift: product changes, competitor changes, market changes. Previous technology cycles hit one at a time. AI is hitting all three simultaneously. That's why companies feel like they're drowning in repositioning work. The specific trap she names: when investors start comparing you to an AI-native competitor, that framing can slide in as your positioning if you're not careful.

> "There's a gap between what feels urgent inside a company versus what's actually urgent to customers."

The discipline she prescribes is the same as always. Go get customer evidence. Map what changed in the product, what closed or opened in the competitive set, and what customers now prioritize differently. Write your new position from that. Not from the VC pitch.
