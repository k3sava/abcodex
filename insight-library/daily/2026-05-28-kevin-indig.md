Kevin Indig published a behavioral study drawing on 846,000 U.S. Google sessions. The finding: AI Mode users accept the answer and move on. AI Overviews users browse and validate. He names the pattern cleanly: AI Mode is autoplay, AI Overviews is the Netflix browse. These are opposite engagement postures, and they need opposite content strategies. Autoplay content needs to be answer-complete and cite-ready -- it terminates the query. Browse content invites follow-on clicks and needs clear hooks. For ab-codex AEO: the operator insight library sits closer to the AI Mode use case -- a practitioner asks a sharp question and wants a sharp answer, sourced. That means every insight page should resolve the query fully rather than tease toward a click. For consulting: clients running SEO programs in 2026 without this distinction in their content briefs are optimizing for the wrong signal.
