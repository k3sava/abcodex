Simon Willison flags a post by Daniel Stenberg (curl's maintainer) describing an unprecedented crush of AI-assisted security issue reports. The volume is unsustainable for a volunteer team. The reports are higher quality than typical human-written ones -- more credible-looking, more thorough -- but the sheer count is overwhelming. The implication extends far past security queues. AI makes credible-looking content cheap to produce at scale. That same pressure hits content moderation, support inboxes, and editorial pipelines. For substrate: timely alongside Anthropic's Claude Security launch the same week -- the offense and defense arrived together. For ab-codex: this is the structural argument for why operator-attributed, verifiable insights hold value. When credible-looking noise is free to produce, provenance becomes the differentiator. For consulting: clients building AI-assisted content workflows need to budget for the review load that volume creates, not just the production savings.
