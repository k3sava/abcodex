TBM 423 asks a question that sounds basic: what is a team? Cutler's answer is that it sounds basic but it isn't, because honest descriptions of team structure surface uncomfortable truths about authority and accountability. People oscillate between three states. Confusion about why the question is hard. A desire for honest description. Resistance when that honesty actually arrives. The post connects to the TBM 422 observation from the same week: "the fatigue is real, the pressure is coming from many directions." Orgs aren't just slow to define teams because of process overhead. They're slow because the real answer names things people aren't ready to name.

For consulting: Any org design engagement that starts with "help us figure out our team structure" is actually asking Cutler's harder question. Clients want the clarity without the discomfort. Naming this pattern early saves time. For ab-codex: The library's value depends on operator insights being honest, not softened. Cutler's observation that honest descriptions meet resistance is the same dynamic ab-codex runs into when a finding is inconvenient for a popular framework. Publish it anyway.
