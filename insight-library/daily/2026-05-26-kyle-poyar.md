Kyle Poyar's May 20 piece cuts against the received wisdom: companies that sell AI tools are adding SDR headcount, not cutting it. The irony is clean. The teams pitching AI-driven automation to buyers still need a human pipeline to close deals. Poyar's framing reframes the "will AI replace SDRs" debate into a more useful question: who actually benefits, and when does human outbound stop being the bottleneck?

For consulting: this is signal for how Kesava frames outbound for clients. The narrative that AI kills SDRs is correct for some categories and wrong for others. Clients selling into enterprise buying committees still need relationship surface. The nuance matters for positioning advice. For career-ops: the human-in-the-loop sales model stays relevant longer than tech-led forecasts suggest, which is actually good for any consultancy that helps clients build GTM motion.
