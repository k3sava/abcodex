April Dunford lays out three triggers that normally shift independently but are now moving together: product changes that unlock new value, competitive changes that close gaps or leapfrog, and market changes that shift buyer priorities. AI is hitting all three simultaneously. The product you shipped 18 months ago does different things today. Rivals have pivoted hard. Buyers carry AI-powered expectations about what software should cost and do. She argues that companies scrambling to reposition right now are not reacting slowly -- they are responding to a genuine triple-change event with no historical precedent. For ab-codex: this trifecta is a clean organizational frame for the insight library. Every operator finding traces to one of these three vectors. For consulting: use it as a positioning audit before any repositioning engagement -- identify which of the three is driving the pressure before prescribing the fix.
