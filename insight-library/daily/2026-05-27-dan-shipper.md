Dan Shipper's TBPN appearance (transcript May 22, 2026) makes two claims worth tracking. First: automation is a lie. Every automated workflow needs a human to catch failures, handle edge cases, and keep the loop from breaking. His company grew headcount despite being AI-forward, precisely because someone has to manage the automation. Second: he revised his earlier prediction. He no longer believes each employee will have their own personal work agent. The new model: one super-agent per company, inside Slack, that every employee routes work through.

For substrate, this shapes the product positioning. Not a suite of per-role AI tools. A single intelligence hub that any team member can hand work to. For consulting, the framing changes from "AI will shrink your team" to "AI moves your people from noise to signal." For career-ops, this is the right frame for Kesava as an operator: someone who builds and runs the systems, not just someone who uses the AI tools.
