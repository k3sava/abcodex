Levie argued in a post and at the Box Content and AI Virtual Summit (May 20) that the shift to AI agents is not just a technology upgrade. Agents, he said, "fundamentally change the underlying workflows of an organization" and directly "rewire the business process itself." That means implementation is not a one-time project. It requires data modernization, access controls, and ongoing maintenance as models evolve. He described this as "an insane amount of technical and domain-specific process work to be done," and predicted the resulting consulting and services boom will exceed the cloud computing transition in scale. The enterprises he surveyed in conversations with 20-plus IT and AI leaders said the biggest blocker is not model quality. It is data fragmentation accumulated over years of siloed systems. For consulting, this is the clearest signal yet that the PMM and positioning opportunity sits at the intersection of agent deployment and organizational change management, not at the model layer. For career-ops, Levie's framing gives Kesava a concrete and quotable macro argument for why PMM+AI consulting is positioned exactly where demand is building.
