Google I/O 2026 on May 19 brought three overlapping search changes covered by Schwartz. The search box itself now expands for long prompts and accepts text, images, files, videos, and Chrome tabs directly. Search is now powered by Gemini 3.5 Flash, deployed the same day it was announced. A May 2026 core update began rolling out May 21. Three structural changes in three days. Schwartz frames the new search box as the biggest change to the interface in 25 years.

For ab-codex and consulting: the search box shift is the AEO inflection point that changes how people reach operator insights. If buyers are now dropping documents and voice clips into a box instead of typing queries, the content model for ab-codex needs to respond. Pages that answer specific questions from specific operators matter more than ever. The Google core update landing simultaneously suggests Google is reweighting content quality signals alongside the interface change.
