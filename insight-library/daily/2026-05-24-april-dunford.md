AI is compressing a positioning crisis that normally unfolds over years into months. April Dunford's May 21 piece argues that companies typically reposition when one of three things shifts: the product unlocks new value, competitors close or open a gap, or the market context changes. AI is moving all three dials at once. The risk she flags is the one that stings most: investor excitement and peer pressure become a substitute for actual customer evidence, so teams shift positioning before they've verified what customers now genuinely need. For Kesava's consulting work, this is a client conversation happening everywhere right now. Helping a founder distinguish "our positioning needs to shift because our customers said X" from "our positioning needs to shift because every VC deck says so" is concrete, billable work. For ab-codex, the insight belongs in the positioning lane as a live case study of why operator-attributed evidence matters more than market narrative.
