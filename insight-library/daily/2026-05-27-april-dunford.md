April Dunford identifies three triggers that force a company to reposition: product changes that unlock new value, competitor moves that close gaps or leapfrog capabilities, and market shifts like regulation, economic disruptions, or technology step-changes. Her argument: AI is hitting all three simultaneously. Companies scramble to update messaging, but she warns against letting investor echo chambers drive the narrative. Listen to actual customer language instead. That is where the real repositioning signal lives.

For Kesava's consulting work, this is the brief that mid-market B2B clients need right now. The PMM pitch shifts from "let us refine your messaging" to "your positioning was built for a market that no longer exists." On ab-codex, Dunford's three-trigger framework belongs as a structural lens on the positioning lane. Every operator insight in that lane could be tagged against which trigger it addresses: product, competitor, or market.
