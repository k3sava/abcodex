Rand Fishkin's argument in two sentences: Google told the web for 25 years to make great content and traffic would follow. Google's AI now consumes that content without sending users back. He calls it "wholesale, intentional, unrepentant content theft." The prescription is blunt. Stop measuring success in traffic. Make things that only you can make. Shift marketing energy to platforms where your audience already pays attention.

For ab-codex, this lands directly. The insight library cannot be a content SEO play. Its value is operator attribution and specificity that AI cannot reproduce or summarize away. A model can abstract Fishkin's framing. It cannot abstract Kesava-sourced intelligence tied to named humans and verified claims. That is the defensible position. For the consulting practice, the same pivot applies: away from content-first GTM, toward platform presence and earned reputation. For substrate, Fishkin's prescription shapes the core product question: what does this tool do that no search result can replace?
