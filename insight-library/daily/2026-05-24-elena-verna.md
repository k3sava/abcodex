Lovable flattened its org in December. Elena Verna, who had been Head of Growth, moved back to solo IC. She kept leader-level pay. She shipped Lovable's enterprise pricing page to production by herself. Her post coins the "HI-IC" (High-Impact Individual Contributor): a solo operator who takes a project from problem to production without needing to coordinate three teams. She frames it as the new career signal, not a step down. For Kesava's career-ops, this is the sharpest articulation yet of the model Kesava is already running across substrate, consulting, and app studio work. The HI-IC framing gives it a name and a narrative. Relevant for any positioning work around "fractional" or "solo operator" consulting, and directly applicable to how ab-codex itself describes the people whose insights it publishes: operators who ship, not managers who coordinate.
