Elena Verna's May 22 post takes the direct line: most tech jobs will look fundamentally different by end of 2027, and passively waiting for clarity is the wrong move. She softened the original headline after reader feedback about its timing during active layoffs, but held the argument. The post is a push for operators to proactively redesign their own roles before market conditions do it for them.

For career-ops and consulting: this is the argument Kesava needs to be able to articulate fluently for any client conversation about GTM team restructuring. The answer is not "AI replaces you" and not "you're safe." It is "the people who choose what AI does to their role will fare better than the people AI chooses for." For ab-codex: insights that help operators make that proactive choice are the library's core value proposition. Verna's framing is a positioning proof point.
