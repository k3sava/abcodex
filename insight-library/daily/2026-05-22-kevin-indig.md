Indig's May 15 brief covers three distinct findings. First: Microsoft's Bing team published a post explaining that AI search inverts the function of the index. When an AI system grounds an answer, the index is no longer pointing to information. It is using it. The source material becomes the answer, not a doorway to one. Second: Ramp ran a 32-day A/B test and killed the schema-first playbook for AI agents. The assumption that structured data markup would give content a direct boost in AI-generated answers did not hold in their test. Third: Amazon built an LLM-powered knowledge graph to surface product relationships across its catalog. These three findings together suggest the same thing: AI systems are not reading pages the way crawlers did. They are consuming signal from structure, authority, and context simultaneously, and no single tactic (structured data alone, links alone, content alone) is sufficient. For ab-codex, this is directly relevant. The insight library's AEO design cannot rely on schema markup as a primary signal. Authority, attribution, and specificity of operator claims are likely to matter more than markup. The Ramp test result is worth sharing as a standalone insight card in the SEO/AEO lane.
