# Codex Thesis

Codex is a primary-source operator insight corpus. Every claim traces to a real operator, a real moment, and a real source.

## What it is
- A Lenny's-style transcript-and-insight library: full-source materials in `raw/`, atomic insights extracted, attributed, and cross-linked.
- Cross-domain by design: PMM, GTM, Product, Design, Engineering, AI-native operating, Leadership, Ops, Research.
- Substrate-shaped: built so any project (personal or team) can cite, link, or ingest from it.

## What it is not
- Not a blog. Not a newsletter. Not commentary on operators — a structured record of what operators said and shipped.
- Not client-specific. Nothing in codex names a specific company's customers, deals, or internal data.
- Not opinion-first. Synthesis pages exist, but they cite atomic insights with sources.

## What it optimizes for
1. **Attribution integrity** — every insight has an operator, a source URL, a date, and a context.
2. **Atomicity** — one claim per card. Composability beats narrative density.
3. **Cross-domain reuse** — a Product insight should be findable from a Marketing query if the underlying mechanism transfers.
4. **Open-corpus durability** — content quality and licensing are clean enough to publish openly.
