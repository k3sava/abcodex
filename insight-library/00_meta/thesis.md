# Codex Thesis

Codex is an open, primary-source operator insight library. Every claim traces to a real operator, a real moment, and a real source.

## What it is
- A library of full-source materials in `raw/` and atomic insights in `insights/`, each attributed, dated, and cross-linked.
- Cross-domain by design: Product, PMM, GTM, Growth, Design, Engineering, AI-native operating, Leadership, Sales, Research, Founder craft.
- Substrate-shaped: any project can read `INDEX.json` and cite or link to cards by id.

## What it is not
- Not a blog. Not a newsletter. Not commentary on operators — a structured record of what operators have published.
- Not opinion-first. Synthesis pages exist, but they cite atomic insights with verifiable sources.

## What it optimizes for
1. **Attribution integrity** — every insight has a named operator, a verifiable source URL, a date, and context.
2. **Atomicity** — one claim per card. Composability beats narrative density.
3. **Cross-domain reuse** — a Product insight should be findable from a Marketing query if the underlying mechanism transfers.
4. **Open-corpus durability** — content quality and licensing are clean enough to publish openly under MIT.
