# A brand's self-promotional "best of" listicle gets cited by AI Overviews 74% of the time but excluded from the recommendation 69% of the time

## Claim
Google AI Overviews have decoupled citation from recommendation: a brand's own self-promotional "best [category] software" listicle appears as a cited source in 74% of tracked queries while that same brand is absent from the actual recommendation in 69% of those citations.

## Mechanism
Google appears to treat self-promotional content as a category signal for the competitors the listicle mentions, not for the publisher. When a brand names rivals in its own "best of" list, those mentions function as editorial votes for those competitors. The publisher's self-citation is discounted as promotional rather than authoritative, while competitor names are extracted as category members worth recommending. The listicle creates AI visibility for rivals at the publisher's expense.

## Conditions
Holds when: the publishing brand has low to moderate authority relative to the category. Third-party reviews and independent comparison pages do not already position the brand as a default recommendation.
Fails when: the brand is already a high-authority category leader. Established brands with strong independent coverage may receive both citation and recommendation from the same listicle.

## Evidence
Lily Ray tracked 100 B2B "best [category] software" queries across three checkpoints (April 15, May 15, June 8, 2026) using Claude and Ahrefs Brand Radar MCP.

> "Across the 80 prompts that triggered an AI Overview (notably, about 1 in 5 'best software' queries didn't surface an AI Overview at all), when a brand's own self-promotional listicle got cited as a source, that brand was left out of the actual recommendation 69% of the time (224 of the 323 self-promotional listicles cited across those prompts) on AI Overviews."

> "Across the three months, 74 of the 100 prompts I tracked (74%) returned an AI Overview answer that cited a self-promoter's own listicle, but left that brand out of the recommendation."

On the competitor-boosting dynamic:

> "While this self-recommendation doesn't influence the AI response, its mentions of competitors Kajabi, Thinkific, LearnWorlds and Teachable may have helped boost them, with all of them earning the AI Overview recommendation."

> "Google appears to have decoupled what it cites from who it recommends."

> "the recommendation is what actually matters — by an order of magnitude"

## Signals
- Your "best of" page appears in AI Overview source footers but your brand is absent from the recommended list in that same answer.
- Competitors you name in your own listicle appear in AI recommendations for queries you tracked.
- Organic ranking on your self-promotional listicle does not correlate with AI recommendation rate.

## Counter-evidence
The sample covers 100 B2B software queries over three months. The effect may not generalize to consumer, e-commerce, or informational query types. Correlation does not confirm causation: brands excluded from recommendations may have pre-existing authority gaps unrelated to the listicle strategy. Ray notes that established brands are partially immune:

> "If you're an established, authoritative brand, you can still publish a listicle calling your brand the best, and you may get both cited and recommended."
