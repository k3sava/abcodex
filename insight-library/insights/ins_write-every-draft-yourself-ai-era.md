# Writing every first draft yourself, then using AI for feedback and fact-checking, preserves the authentic perspective that AI curation systems select for

## Claim
When AI systems evaluate content for recommendation, writing every first draft yourself before using AI for feedback and fact-checking creates the authentic perspective signal that distinguishes the work from AI-generated uniformity.

## Mechanism
AI-generated content is undifferentiated by default because models trained on similar corpora produce similar outputs. The first draft is where the author's actual reasoning, distinctive framing, and non-obvious positions are established. AI tools can refine, fact-check, and unblock the writer without replacing that foundation, because they are reacting to the author's choices rather than generating the choices themselves. Mollick applied this structure explicitly: he authored every chapter, then used AI to check facts and read for feedback. The author's first-draft thinking is what makes the resulting work worth recommending.

## Conditions
Holds when: the content domain rewards expertise and perspective over volume; the author has formed views from direct experience; AI recommendation is a meaningful discovery path for the intended audience.

Fails when: the output is purely informational (specifications, data summaries, reference tables) and perspective adds no signal. When speed and volume are the primary objectives, first-draft authorship is impractical at scale.

## Evidence
Mollick describes his workflow for writing Co-Existence:

> "I wrote every chapter draft myself."

He adds the refinement layer:

> "I had AI readers go through each chapter for feedback...I had a council of AI models check my facts."

The structure is deliberate: human at origination, AI in the refinement loop. The rationale is that this sequence preserves voice, which matters when AI systems are the recommendation gateway.

## Signals
- The author can defend every position in the work without referencing the AI tool used.
- AI assistants, when asked to summarize the work, capture a distinctive viewpoint rather than averaging domain consensus.
- The work passes a "surprise test": it contains at least one position a generic AI model would not have generated from the same prompt.

## Counter-evidence
This workflow depends on the author having formed views worth preserving. It is impractical for high-volume or team-produced content. Some AI-first workflows deliberately use AI to generate diverse seed positions as a starting point, then curate; that approach optimizes for breadth and speed rather than depth and authenticity. Neither is universally superior.
