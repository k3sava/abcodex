# B2B buyers now research vendors in AI tools before any sales contact, making AI-search discoverability a pre-funnel requirement

## Claim
B2B buyers complete most of their research through ChatGPT, Claude, Reddit, and peer networks before talking to any vendor. By the time an SDR email arrives, the buyer has already formed opinions about the category and the competitive set. Being findable and citable in AI-generated answers is now a precondition for demand generation, not an optional channel add-on.

## Mechanism
Traditional demand generation assumes the buyer first encounters the brand through the vendor's own channels: ads, content, outbound. AI changes the order. A buyer who opens ChatGPT and asks "what tools do teams use for X?" receives a synthesized answer that names vendors before the buyer has visited any vendor's site. First impressions now form inside the AI's response. If a vendor is not cited there, it does not exist at the consideration stage. Buyers who arrive at a demo call have already self-qualified against AI-curated alternatives, which compresses early sales conversations and raises the bar for what the demo must do.

Gerhardt frames these buyers as "anti-social" because they avoid vendor contact during research. The implication for demand gen teams is that gating content, requiring form fills, or relying on volume outbound all underperform when the buyer's research phase has already closed before contact.

## Conditions
Holds when: the product category is established enough that AI models have training data on it; buyers have access to AI chat tools during their research phase; the deal has enough complexity that buyers seek outside information before committing.
Fails when: the product is so new that AI models have no knowledge of the category; the buyer is repeat-purchasing a commodity with no research phase; the sale is transactional and impulse-driven.

## Evidence
Gerhardt's claim is based on pattern observation across the Exit Five community (B2B marketing practitioners) and customer interviews. No controlled study is cited. He named the buyer pattern and named the implication for demand generation strategy.

> "Your buyer already knows about your product. They asked ChatGPT."

## Signals
- Demo request forms show buyers who have already evaluated two to three named competitors, identified unprompted.
- Outbound reply rates drop while inbound from AI-referred queries or branded search rises.
- Prospects reference AI-generated category summaries in early sales calls without prompting.

## Counter-evidence
Gerhardt's framing is based on community observation and interviews, not a controlled study of buyer behavior at scale. The "anti-social buyer" pattern is most likely true in mid-market and enterprise B2B software deals where research cycles are long. It may not apply to SMB purchases, impulse-driven tools, or categories where buyers rely heavily on peer referrals rather than AI tools. The mechanism also assumes AI models surface the vendor accurately; categories with heavy AI-overview suppression or misinformation may not follow this pattern.
