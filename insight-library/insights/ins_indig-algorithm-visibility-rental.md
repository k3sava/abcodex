# Search visibility earned through algorithm shifts reverts to baseline unless underlying authority supports it

## Claim
Google reallocates search shelf space within stable demand categories without creating new demand. Sites that gain visibility from a core update without a corresponding gain in authority will lose it in the next update cycle; sites that hold authority-backed positions retain them.

## Mechanism
Aggregate click volume for a search category stays flat when user demand for that category has not changed. The total shelf space available is fixed. When an algorithm update shifts who occupies those positions, it is a reallocation, not an expansion. A site that gains positions because it temporarily matched a new ranking signal pattern does not hold the underlying authority indicators that justify the rank on merit. When Google refines or corrects the signal, the rank reverts. A site with deep, earned authority holds its positions across updates because each update is a refinement of the same underlying signals: intent match, engagement, link quality, and trust.

## Conditions
Holds when: aggregate category demand is flat or declining; the site gained visibility from an update, not from genuine improvements to authority signals.
Fails when: the category is experiencing real demand growth, in which case authority-thin sites can sustain positions by riding expanding category volume. Also fails when a site made genuine authority improvements that happened to coincide with an update.

## Evidence
Indig's data window covers May 11 to June 8, 2026 (28 days), with year-over-year comparison.

Macy's gained visibility from roughly 48.8 to 102.0 in March 2026, then dropped 53.8% over four weeks to near its February baseline by June 8, 2026. Yelp, by contrast, posted its all-time visibility high (646.5 on the Sistrix scale), driving a 30.5% lift across the local search vertical. Yelp's gain is authority-backed; Macy's was not.

Indig names the pattern directly:

> "Algorithm-gifted visibility is a rental."

> "Google reallocates shelf space within categories whose demand hasn't changed."

## Signals
- A site's visibility jumps after a core update without a corresponding increase in domain authority, earned links, or brand search volume.
- The visibility gain reverses fully or partially within two to three update cycles.
- Competitors with stronger underlying authority signals reclaim the positions that the site temporarily held.

## Counter-evidence
Not every algorithm-driven gain is a rental. Some core updates correct sites that were previously under-rewarded for genuine authority, and those corrections are permanent. The rental framework applies when the gain lacks a corresponding authority signal, not as a blanket rule for all update-driven movement. Indig's own data shows Yelp gaining and holding, demonstrating that the market correctly rewards durable authority.
