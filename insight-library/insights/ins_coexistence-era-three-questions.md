# The co-existence era replaces co-intelligence and surfaces three new judgment questions

## Claim
The era of interactive human-and-chatbot collaboration ("co-intelligence") has ended; autonomous coding agents and AI systems that outperform humans in many tasks define a new "co-existence" era, which demands three new judgment questions: when to refuse AI's help, when to hand over full control, and how to operate when AI is the gatekeeper between your work and its audience.

## Mechanism
AI companies never targeted cooperative tools. The stated goal, per the OpenAI charter Mollick cites, was "highly autonomous systems that outperform humans at most economically valuable work." Late 2025 made that concrete: one study found coding agents produced seventeen times more code; Anthropic reported AI now writes 80% of its code, with developers shipping eight times more. When AI is sometimes genuinely better than the human and the human knows it, the framing of "how do I use this tool" breaks down. The operative question shifts to "when should I use this at all." That is a judgment call that cannot be automated.

## Conditions
Holds when: AI systems operate with genuine autonomy on tasks that produce real outputs (code shipped, content published, decisions made without human review).
Fails when: AI outputs are always reviewed before use, preserving the old co-intelligence loop intact.

## Evidence
Mollick tracked the arrival of actual coding agents in late 2025 and his own experience optimizing his new book for AI readers as a distinct audience. He built a dedicated webpage for AI agents to evaluate before recommending the book to humans, ran A/B tests across dozens of AI models via Codex, and used multiple models as reviewers and fact-checkers. Each of those actions was a response to AI serving as gatekeeper, not assistant.

> "working with AI that is sometimes better than you, and sometimes hilariously worse, is not a problem you solve once"

> "When should you refuse AI's help, even when it is offering? When should you hand over the keys entirely?"

## Signals
- Your team ships code or content faster than it can read or verify the output.
- You are optimizing anything (web pages, pitches, documentation) for AI model readers rather than just human readers.
- A meaningful fraction of your outputs have no human in the review loop.

## Counter-evidence
The 80% AI-written code figure is specific to Anthropic's engineering team, an unusually AI-forward context. Most knowledge workers are still in early adoption. Mollick acknowledges AI remains "not a great long-form writer," and the three new questions apply unevenly across domains.
