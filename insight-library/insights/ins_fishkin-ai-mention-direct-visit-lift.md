# AI brand recommendations produce a 7 to 14 percent direct visit lift within seven days

## Claim
When an AI tool recommends a brand, visitors arrive directly at the brand's domain within seven days at measurably higher rates, with branded search volume falling slightly in the same window.

## Mechanism
An AI recommendation satisfies the discovery need inside the conversation itself. The user already has enough context to act, so they navigate directly to the domain rather than re-querying a search engine. The search engine is bypassed entirely. This shifts attribution from branded-search to direct-traffic, which makes the lift invisible to teams tracking search share of voice alone.

## Conditions
Holds when: the brand is recognizable enough that users can navigate directly after hearing the name. Strongest for financial, retail, and consumer brands where the domain name is predictable. The window is short: effects appear and peak within seven days of the AI mention.

Fails when: the brand is obscure or has a non-obvious domain, forcing users to search anyway. Also less applicable to B2B products where the decision cycle is long and a single AI mention is rarely enough to trigger a direct visit.

## Evidence
SparkToro and Similarweb analyzed traffic patterns around AI tool recommendations for large consumer brands. The findings:

> "if Capital One gets recommended...by an AI tool, it is quite likely that someone will directly go to their browser and type in your domain name."

American Express visitors were 7.2% more likely to arrive directly when AmEx was recommended by an AI tool. Capital One visitors were 14.2% more likely. Across brands studied, branded search volume fell roughly 15% in the same window. The effect materialized within seven days of the AI mention.

## Signals
- Direct traffic rises without any paid or organic search change.
- Branded search volume dips at the same time direct traffic rises.
- The pattern repeats when a brand earns AI placements in new categories or tools.

## Counter-evidence
The research covers large, recognizable consumer brands. Whether the same direct-visit lift occurs for mid-market B2B SaaS or niche indie brands is not established. The 7-day window also assumes users act quickly; for high-consideration purchases, the intent may dissolve before a visit occurs. Correlation between AI mention and direct visit does not rule out confounding (brands that earn AI mentions may also be running other demand-generation activity simultaneously).
