# AI Overviews collapse all five search intent types into a single reading session, making behavioral segmentation across intents obsolete

## Claim
AI Overviews flatten search behavior across navigational, informational, commercial, transactional, and local intents, pulling every searcher into an extended reading session regardless of what they came to find.

## Mechanism
A traditional SERP hands off to the destination quickly. Users with navigational intent click fast; users with informational intent linger. AIOs break this pattern by injecting a detailed answer at the top. That answer satisfies enough of the initial query that even navigational and transactional searchers begin reading rather than clicking. SERP retention at 21 seconds ranged from 12% to 32% across intent types before AIOs. With AIOs present, all five types cluster at 41.9% to 48.5%. The 20-point variance that underpinned intent-segmented content strategies collapses to a 7-point band. Sessions are approximately 4x longer. Twenty years of SEO content segmentation was built on that variance.

## Conditions
Holds when: AIOs appear in the SERP for the query type in question. 1.5 billion users globally now see AIOs on a significant share of queries.
Fails when: the query is brand-navigational with no AIO trigger; the user is on a device or locale where AIOs are not shown; or the user has learned to skip the AIO block.

## Evidence
Analysis of 846,000 anonymized U.S.-based Google search sessions. Without AIO: retention at 21 seconds ranged from 12% (navigational) to 32% (local). With AIO present: retention across all five intent types clustered at 41.9% to 48.5%. Average session length approximately 4x longer.

Kevin Indig frames the structural shift directly:

> "Intent compression breaks the segmentation SEO ran on for 20 years."

## Signals
- Click-through rates converging across previously distinct intent categories.
- SERP session duration rising even for queries that previously showed short-click behavior.
- Content mapped to navigational intents underperforming retention benchmarks built before AIOs.

## Counter-evidence
The dataset covers U.S.-based sessions at one point in AIO rollout. Behavior may differ by device, language, or category. Some operators report that brand-navigational queries still send users to the destination without notable AIO interference. If users learn to skip the AIO block habitually, the compression effect weakens. The study covers one window in time and may not reflect long-run behavioral equilibrium.
