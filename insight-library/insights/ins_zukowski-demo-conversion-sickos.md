# A 19 percent demo-to-wishlist conversion rate is the expected median; demos target hardcore discovery players, not mass wishlisting

## Claim
The typical game demo converts only 19.3% of players to wishlists, and this is the expected, healthy outcome. Demos work by converting a small cohort of hardcore discovery players who then market the game to a broader audience, not by converting the majority of demo players to wishlists.

## Mechanism
Steam demos attract a disproportionate share of "Sickos" - hardcore players who play every demo looking for hidden gems ahead of the crowd. These players rarely wishlist even games they love, but they create YouTube content, write forum posts, and recommend games to friends outside Steam. The word-of-mouth generated by this cohort reaches an audience that would never have played the demo. Optimizing for wishlist conversion rate misunderstands the mechanism: a demo that converts 50% of a low-intent mass audience is less valuable than one that engages 15% of a high-intent Sicko cohort that then becomes a marketing multiplier.

## Conditions
Holds when: the game has genuine quality that appeals to the Sicko cohort; the developer has tagged and positioned the game correctly so hardcore discovery players find it.
Fails when: the demo is the only marketing touchpoint with no community, streamers, or press to amplify Sicko word-of-mouth; or when the game lacks qualities that Sickos discuss publicly (niche, experimental, or deliberately low-visibility genres).

## Evidence
Zukowski surveyed Steam developers and found a median demo-to-wishlist conversion rate of 19.3%. 70th percentile: 23.2%. 30th percentile: 15.9%. One specific data point: a game with 713 demo plays converted 175 to wishlists (24.5%).

> "You are making a demo for them [Sickos]. You market to Sickos and then if they like you, they market your game to their friends."

> "Do not make gameplay changes based on this data."

## Signals
- Forum posts, YouTube videos, or Discord discussion appear about your demo from players who did not wishlist.
- Demo play counts outpace wishlist additions by 4:1 to 7:1, but pre-launch community activity grows regardless.
- Streamers and content creators engage with the demo without having wishlisted it first.

## Counter-evidence
The Sicko effect requires the Sickos to genuinely like and talk about the game. A low-quality demo generates no word-of-mouth regardless of conversion rate. The 19.3% median is an aggregate across all genres; games in highly popular genres may see higher rates because casual players wishlist more readily. The mechanism also assumes some external channel (YouTube, forums, Discord) exists for Sicko word-of-mouth to travel through.
