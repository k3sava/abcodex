# Help Scout has run fully remote since 2011 and serves 12,000+ teams over 950 million customer conversations

## Claim
Nick Francis, Jared McDaniel, and Denny Swindle founded Help Scout in 2011 as a customer-support software company built around the conviction that "all customers deserve to be treated like people." The team has been fully remote from inception, currently working from over 115 cities globally, and the platform has processed 950+ million customer conversations for 12,000+ customer-facing teams.

## Mechanism
A help-desk product is a forcing function for the company's own customer-experience standards: if the team that builds the tool treats users like ticket numbers, the product reflects it. Hiring fully remote from inception removes the office-vs-remote two-tier culture problem that hits later-stage remote conversions. Founders staying in operating roles for 14+ years compounds the original taste into the product.

## Conditions
Holds when the founders' personal customer-experience standard is the product's standard.
Fails when the company scales the support team faster than the product can absorb context, producing the same templated experience the founders set out to avoid.

## Signals
- Founders still operating the company 14+ years in.
- Distributed-first hiring (115+ cities).
- Customer-experience standards consistent across years (no "we used to be different" pivots).

## Counter-evidence
Customer-support software is a category with mature open-source alternatives and well-funded incumbents (Zendesk, Intercom). Help Scout's calm posture undertrades the marketing budget needed to win against subsidized competitors. Reproducing the model in a more capital-intensive category is harder.
