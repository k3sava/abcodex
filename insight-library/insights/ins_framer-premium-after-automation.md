# AI automation creates more demand for human framers, not less, because models cannot decide what problems matter now

## Claim
Heavy AI automation of a company's entire workflow paradoxically increases the need for human judgment: by commoditizing the residue of past expertise into abundant, interchangeable output, AI creates a market for the one thing it cannot provide, which is contact with the present situation as it actually exists.

## Mechanism
Shipper calls this the framer-executor distinction. An executor operates within a defined frame: a set of constraints, goals, and quality criteria that can be specified in advance. AI excels at execution because prior human work provides the training data to define those frames. A framer is someone in contact with the whole situation, including what the frame necessarily discards: the client's unstated priorities, the market signal that surfaced this week, the quality standard that just shifted. Models operate on past data; framers operate on present reality. When AI commoditizes execution, output becomes abundant and uniform. Buyers then select on differentiation and freshness, which only framers can supply. Every expansion in AI execution capacity raises the floor of what counts as acceptable output, which raises the threshold at which human framing becomes the differentiator.

## Conditions
Holds when: the domain has observable quality standards that buyers can use to distinguish human-framed outputs from AI-commoditized outputs.
Fails when: the domain has no established quality bar and volume is the only metric (pure content arbitrage plays, for example).

## Evidence
Shipper runs Every, a media and software company that grew from four employees to thirty since GPT-3 while automating everything it could automate, including coding, writing, design, and customer service.

> "There's more human work to do than ever"

> "AI commoditizes the residue of human expertise...creating demand for what's different."

> "The frame is not the framer"

His distinction: models can execute within a frame but cannot decide what problems matter now, because that requires situational contact with the present, which models lack by construction.

## Signals
- Your most valuable contributors are spending more time on problem selection and brief-writing than on execution.
- AI-generated output is indistinguishable from competitor AI-generated output, and buyers are commenting on the sameness.
- The work your best people do that AI cannot replicate is increasingly the work that closes deals.

## Counter-evidence
Not all work scales this way. Some execution domains have a high enough quality bar that AI output remains differentiated from mediocre human output, temporarily reducing the framing premium. And in early-stage companies where speed beats quality, framing overhead can be a liability.
