# CTV gives B2B brands 15 to 30 seconds of uninterrupted narrative that compounds downstream channel performance

## Claim
Connected TV gives B2B marketers a 15 to 30 second window of uninterrupted narrative time that builds brand recognition, which then amplifies branded search, cold outbound, and email performance.

## Mechanism
Digital channels compete for scroll-stop attention, cutting story time to fractions of a second. CTV creates a captive audience window without the interruption problem, allowing a full story to land. The brand familiarity built through repeated exposure becomes the compounding asset: recipients of cold outbound and email recognize the brand before any direct contact, reducing friction in the first response. Branded search grows as familiarity increases. The compounding is not immediate; it builds through repetition over months. Amit Sharan (SVP Marketing, Tatari) cites 25 percent average improvement in branded search performance across clients running CTV alongside search and outbound.

## Conditions
Holds when: The company has an existing retargetable audience (roughly 10,000 monthly website visitors or an equivalent LinkedIn audience), runs the channel consistently over months rather than as a single burst, and uses creative that tells a story rather than listing features.
Fails when: Creative is promotional rather than narrative, the audience is too small to retarget effectively, or the channel runs for fewer than 60 to 90 days before being evaluated.

## Evidence
Gerhardt, Exit Five newsletter #267, July 14, 2026:

> "Where else do you get 15-30 uninterrupted seconds to tell your story?"

> "A channel that compounds instead of a one-off campaign."

> "People buy from the brands they know, so make sure they hear about you, again and again."

Sharan's operational data from Tatari: minimum $50 per day to start, 25 percent average branded search lift after running CTV alongside other channels, over $100 million in client CTV spend tracked by Tatari.

## Signals
- Branded search volume increases in the two to three months following consistent CTV spend.
- Cold email reply rates improve without copy changes, attributed to pre-existing brand familiarity.
- Inbound leads mention having "seen you everywhere" despite what would appear to be narrow total reach.

## Counter-evidence
The 25 percent branded search lift is an average from Tatari's client base, not a controlled trial, so attribution is hard to isolate. Brands with weak narrative creative, inconsistent messaging, or very small total addressable audiences may not see the effect. Very niche B2B markets with a small ICP limit reach: if the ideal customer profile numbers only a few thousand people, CTV inventory is difficult to target with sufficient precision.
