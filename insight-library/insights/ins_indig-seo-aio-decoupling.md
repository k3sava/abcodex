# Google's organic SEO and AI Overview citations now move in opposite directions, requiring separate strategies

## Claim
In June 2026, Google's May core update boosted the five largest mega-platforms by 18 to 43% in organic SEO visibility while AI Overviews simultaneously cited those same platforms 2 to 13% less. The two systems now score content on different criteria and can move in opposing directions.

## Mechanism
Google appears to operate two distinct ranking systems with different scoring signals. The organic index rewards engagement volume, trust breadth, and platform authority. AI Overviews reward source diversity, original data, and authoritative reference content. A site that wins on platform scale may fail AI citation criteria precisely because it is ubiquitous rather than authoritative on a specific topic. The May 2026 core update made this split visible for the first time at scale: the same sites gained organic shelf space and lost AI citation share in the same four-week window.

## Conditions
Holds when: a brand measures organic SEO visibility and AI Overview citation share separately and finds them moving in different directions.
Fails when: a brand's content type (original benchmarks, primary research, expert reference content) satisfies both systems simultaneously. High-authority primary sources may maintain portability across both.

## Evidence
Indig analyzed platform performance over the four-week window ending June 29, 2026:

- LinkedIn: +43.3% organic SEO visibility, minus 2.0% AI Overview mentions
- Reddit: +18.1% visibility, minus 12.8% AI mentions
- Facebook: +20.1% visibility, minus 12.1% AI mentions
- Instagram: +21.4% visibility, minus 9.7% AI mentions

> "SEO visibility and AI citation used to drift together for the mega-platforms. In June, they came apart."

> "The platforms Google just rewarded in organic are the same ones AI Overviews cited less often in June. That split is the story of the month."

## Signals
- Organic dashboards show ranking gains while AI citation tracking shows flat or declining brand mentions.
- A single content strategy optimization improves one metric at the expense of the other.
- Pages that rank top 3 organically rarely appear in AI Overview citations on the same query.

## Counter-evidence
One month of divergence data does not establish a permanent structural split. The May 2026 core update may be a temporary adjustment that partially reverses. Mega-platforms are a specific content type; the pattern may not generalize to mid-tier publishers or niche sites. Aggregate organic visibility is not the same as ranking on specific commercial queries, which may show a different pattern.
