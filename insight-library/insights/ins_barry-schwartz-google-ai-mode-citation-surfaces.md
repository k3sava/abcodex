# Google formalized five citation surfaces in AI Mode and AI Overviews, giving AEO a specific placement target inside Google search

## Claim
Google deployed five link-placement features in AI Mode and AI Overviews: links adjacent to relevant text, "explore further" end-sections, trusted subscription source highlights, creator context on discussion links, and desktop hover previews. AEO practitioners now have specific structural targets inside Google, not just third-party LLMs.

## Mechanism
Google's AI Mode previously placed links inconsistently. Formalizing placement means the relationship between page structure and citation position is now engineered rather than emergent. Pages optimized for structural prominence (headers, footer, nav signals) have a predictable path to visible citation inside Google AI responses.

## Conditions
Holds when: the page is crawled and indexed; when structural prominence has been established before the citation surfaces mature.
Fails when: the page is paywalled or carries noindex tags; when the content category falls under Google's quality restrictions.

## Evidence
Barry Schwartz, Search Engine Roundtable, May 7:

> "Google rolled out five link-improvement features in AI Mode and AI Overviews."

The five features: (1) links placed closer to relevant text in responses, (2) "explore further" suggestions at answer-end, (3) trusted subscription source highlights, (4) creator context on discussion links, (5) desktop hover previews. Previously experimental, now formally deployed.

## Signals
- Impressions appear in Google Search Console from AI Mode queries
- Click-through rate from AI Mode citations improves after a structural prominence pass
- "Explore further" end-section includes your site for target category queries

## Counter-evidence
These surfaces are in early rollout; full global deployment timeline is unconfirmed. Citation behavior in AI Mode may evolve faster than page optimization cycles can match.
