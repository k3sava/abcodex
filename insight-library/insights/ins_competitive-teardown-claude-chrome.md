# AI clears the runway for competitive strategy, 8-10 hour teardowns compressed to under an hour

## Claim
Competitive teardowns that historically required 8-10 hours of multi-channel analysis (website, pricing, reviews, social, demos) can be compressed to under an hour using Claude Chrome for multi-channel scraping + synthesis. The unlock isn't AI replacing the analyst, it's AI clearing the manual data-gathering tax so the analyst's time goes to the strategic interpretation that actually matters.

## Mechanism
Manual teardowns burn most of the time on data acquisition: navigating sites, screenshotting features, copy-pasting reviews, transcribing pricing pages. Claude Chrome extracts and synthesizes across tabs in minutes. The analyst's remaining work, what does this competitive set tell us about positioning, product roadmap, message gaps, is the high-leverage interpretation. PMMs who systematically use AI to clear the data tax produce more teardowns more often, which compounds into faster competitive intelligence.

## Conditions
Holds when:
- The competitive set has accessible online presence (websites, public reviews, demos).
- The PMM has the strategic capability to interpret synthesized output.

Fails when:
- Stealth or pre-launch competitors with limited public footprint.
- Teardowns requiring proprietary data (signed-up product trials, internal demos).

## Signals
- PMM produces teardowns weekly or monthly, not quarterly.

## Counter-evidence
AI-synthesized output has known failure modes (hallucinated features, miscategorized pricing) that require human verification before the time-savings actually land. Some categories also rely on offline / sales-call competitive intelligence that AI can't access.
