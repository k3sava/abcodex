# Third-party sources generate 84 to 93 percent of AI citation weight for SaaS brands across platforms

## Claim
Third-party sources generate between 84 and 93 percent of AI citation weight for SaaS brands, varying by subvertical and platform, making the external content ecosystem the primary AEO channel rather than owned site content.

## Mechanism
AI systems synthesize answers from wherever authoritative context lives, not from where brands choose to publish. For SaaS brands, authority signals cluster in peer software review sites, technical communities, and video content, not on owned websites. Owned site content contributes only 6 to 16 percent of citations. The specific mix also varies by platform: ChatGPT leans on structured written sources, with peer software vendors accounting for roughly 25 percent of head mentions and video just one percent. AI Mode inverts this, giving video and social content roughly 23 percent of weight. A uniform AEO playbook fails because the two platforms build answers from different evidence ecosystems.

## Conditions
Holds when: A SaaS brand relies primarily on owned content as its AEO strategy and has not yet built a strong external ecosystem matching the platform's citation preferences.
Fails when: The brand has already invested in peer review profiles, technical documentation in third-party communities, and video presence at the scale each platform rewards.

## Evidence
Solis, writing July 16, 2026, based on analysis across three SaaS subverticals:

> "Most AI citation weight comes from outside your own website"

> "Publishing authoritative content on your own site is necessary but rarely sufficient."

> "The two platforms build answers from different evidence ecosystems: ChatGPT leans on structured written sources; AI Mode gives far greater prominence to video, creator and social content."

The 84 to 93 percent third-party figure spans CRM, collaboration, and finance subverticals. Peer software vendors are the single largest category in ChatGPT (~25 percent of head mentions). A further complication: AI traffic outside cited URLs ranges from 22 to 67 percent by category, meaning brands receive AI-driven visits from pages that never generated a citation.

## Signals
- A brand with strong owned content but weak third-party presence sees low AI citation rates despite high on-site quality scores.
- AI-driven traffic arrives at pages not predicted by the brand's citation tracking tool.
- Branded search share differs between ChatGPT and AI Mode users, signaling different evidence sources at work.

## Counter-evidence
The citation weight data reflects AI systems as they stood at time of publication, not a permanent structure. As AI systems update retrieval weights and training data, the balance between owned and third-party sources may shift. The 22 to 67 percent of AI traffic arriving at non-cited URLs also suggests AI systems surface owned content without citing it explicitly, which complicates any strategy built purely on citation maximization.
