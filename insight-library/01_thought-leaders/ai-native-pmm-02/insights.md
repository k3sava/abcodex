# Andy Raskin Insights

## Insight: Narrative must define the change, not just describe the product
- Leader: Andy Raskin
- Domain: PMM / Sales Enablement
- Maturity: Foundational
- Confidence: Medium

### Claim
Great B2B narratives organize around a consequential market change and the stakes of inaction, then position the product as the inevitable response.

### Why it matters
Without this structure, AI-generated messaging becomes feature soup instead of decision-driving communication.

### Mechanism
Anchor all messaging assets to one strategic narrative spine: change, stakes, promised land, proof, and plan.

### Apply when
- Messaging is clear but not compelling.
- Executive buyers understand features but don’t feel urgency.

### Evidence signals to track
- Executive meeting conversion rate.
- Sales-cycle acceleration in mid/late stages.
- Message recall in win/loss interviews.

### OS mapping
- Commons: Narrative Spine artifact.
- Flywheel: Stage-based narrative resonance metrics.
- clearPMM: Strategic narrative workshop routine.
