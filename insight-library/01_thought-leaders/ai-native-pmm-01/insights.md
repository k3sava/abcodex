# April Dunford Insights

## Insight: Positioning as an explicit decision stack
- Leader: April Dunford
- Domain: PMM / Sales Enablement
- Maturity: Foundational
- Confidence: High

### Claim
Strong positioning is a sequence of clear decisions (competitive alternatives, unique attributes, value themes, target segment, category frame), not a tagline exercise.

### Why it matters
Your Commons substrate can store each decision as a first-class artifact, preventing random drift in downstream messaging.

### Mechanism
Capture each positioning decision with timestamp, owner, and evidence. Then generate persona-specific story variants without changing core strategic choices.

### Apply when
- Messaging keeps changing by stakeholder preference.
- Sales and marketing use different language for the same product.

### Evidence signals to track
- Win-rate delta after narrative alignment.
- Reduction in sales call “what do you actually do?” confusion.

### OS mapping
- Commons: Positioning Decision Log.
- Flywheel: Predicted impact by segment + win/loss tracking.
- clearPMM: Reusable positioning sprint workflow.

## Insight: Positioning must define the segment-specific value proof
- Leader: April Dunford
- Domain: PMM
- Maturity: Applied
- Confidence: High

### Claim

### Mechanism

### Apply when

### Don’t apply when

### Evidence signals to track
- Stage-3 to close conversion.

### OS mapping

- Flywheel: Proof usage vs win-rate.
- clearPMM: Proof design sprint.
