# Rory Woodbridge Insights

## Insight: PMM leverage comes from systems, not heroic one-off outputs
- Leader: Rory Woodbridge
- Domain: PMM / GTM
- Maturity: Applied
- Confidence: Medium

### Claim
The highest-leverage PMM teams operate with reusable frameworks, clear cadences, and artifact discipline rather than ad hoc launch heroics.

### Why it matters
This directly aligns with your thesis that substrate quality determines compounding outcomes.

### Mechanism
Codify recurring PMM motions (positioning refresh, launch prep, enablement updates) into checklists + templates, then measure deltas over time.

### Apply when
- Team quality depends on individual memory.
- Launch outcomes vary wildly by owner.

### Evidence signals to track
- Time-to-launch readiness.
- Rework rate across messaging assets.
- Sales confidence before/after enablement drops.

### OS mapping
- Commons: PMM motion templates with version history.
- Flywheel: Predicted outcome + post-mortem loop per launch.
- clearPMM: Personal PMM operating cadence stack.

## Insight: PMM should own decision interfaces, not every artifact
- Leader: Rory Woodbridge
- Domain: PMM / GTM
- Maturity: Applied
- Confidence: Medium

### Claim
PMM leverage increases when PMM designs decision interfaces across teams rather than trying to personally produce every asset.

### Mechanism
Define required inputs/outputs for launches and narrative shifts, then route execution to specialists with QA checkpoints.

### Apply when
- PMM is a bottleneck.

### Don’t apply when
- Foundational strategy is still undefined.

### Evidence signals to track
- Decision cycle time, cross-team handoff quality.

### OS mapping
- Commons: Decision interface templates.
- Flywheel: Handoff quality scorecards.
- clearPMM: Delegation and QA routines.
