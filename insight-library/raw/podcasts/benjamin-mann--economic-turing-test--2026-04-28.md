# Benjamin Mann — Anthropic co-founder

## Why this is in the wiki
Mann is a frontier-AI insider talking openly about timelines (~2028 50th-percentile super-intelligence), the Economic Turing Test for transformative AI, the right way to use new tools, and Anthropic's mission-led talent retention against $100M Meta poaching. Three direct loads for Kesava: (a) the Economic Turing Test is a clean metric for the flywheel substrate's revenue-per-FTE pitch, (b) the "ambitious + retry-from-scratch" usage pattern is the operating discipline already encoded in Kesava's flywheel substrate work, (c) the constitutional AI / values-aligned personality argument is the strongest external case for the operator-voice + kill-list discipline as a brand asset, not a style preference.

## 11 load-bearing insights

1. **The Economic Turing Test as the AGI benchmark.** "If you contract an agent for a month or three months on a particular job, if you decide to hire that agent and it turns out to be a machine rather than a person, then it's passed the Economic Turing Test for that role." Expand to a money-weighted basket of jobs; 50% threshold = transformative AI. Direct read for the flywheel pitch: don't argue about whether AI is "smart enough" in general; ask whether the substrate would pass the Economic Turing Test for a specific marketing workstream. That's the right metric for the CXO demo.

2. **Scaling laws are not slowing down. The releases are just more frequent.** "Progress has actually been accelerating. The cadence of model releases used to be once a year and now... every month or three months." Comparison fatigue masks acceleration. Time-dilation effect. Cross-link to Sherwin Wu's "this is the worst the models will ever be" and Boris Cherny's "build for the next model." Same operating principle: don't ship the substrate scaffolding around current model limits; assume the limits dissolve.

3. **Some tasks saturate; we need better benchmarks.** When new benchmarks ship, they get saturated within 6-12 months. "The real constraint is how can we come up with better benchmarks and better ambition of using the tools that then reveals the bumps in intelligence." Direct read for evals work (Hamel/Shreya): the moving frontier is application-specific evals, not generic benchmarks. The flywheel substrate's eval suite IS the local benchmark.

4. **Customer service: 82% resolution at Fin/Intercom, fully automated.** The 18% that needs humans is the harder half. "The humans doing those tasks are able to focus on the harder parts." Direct external precedent for the flywheel substrate: marketing analog target is 80%+ structural pass-through (drafts, variants, briefs, structural reviews) handled by substrate; humans focus on the 20% taste-and-decision work. Use this benchmark in the CXO pitch — same shape, different domain.

5. **95% of Claude Code's code is written by Claude. Output is 10-20x.** Same shape Sherwin Wu reports inside OpenAI. The compounding pattern is real. Direct read for the flywheel: marketing-team output should target 10-20x compounding through the substrate, not 1.5x productivity gain. Set ambition accordingly in the pitch math.

6. **Use new tools as new tools, not as old tools.** "People who use the new tools as if they were old tools tend to not succeed." Specific example: Claude Code users who ask for ambitious changes and retry the prompt 3+ times outperform those who try once and bang on the same failure. Direct mandate for the flywheel team: ambitious prompting, retry-from-scratch when stuck, don't iterate on broken context. Match Kesava's substrate-iteration cadence.

7. **The legal and finance teams at Anthropic use Claude Code.** Not just engineers. "They use it to redline documents and run BigQuery analyses of our customers and our revenue metrics." Direct read for clearPMM positioning: PMM is on the same trajectory as legal + finance + engineering — workflow collapse, not workflow speedup. The substrate is not a marketing-only product; it's how every operator does work.

8. **Mission > money, and Anthropic has measurably less attrition.** "My best case scenario at Meta is that we make money, and my best case at Anthropic is we affect the future of humanity." $100M poaching offers stick less when the mission is clearly higher-leverage. Direct read for the flywheel narrative — the team-six framing matters because it answers "why are you here?" not just "what do you do?" Useful for Kesava's exit-clock conversation: the mission frame survives the comp frame when the comp comp gets matched.

9. **AGI is loaded; "transformative AI" is the better term.** Less about "can it do everything?" More about "is it causing measurable economic and social transformation?" Same shape as the operator vs. agentic-SaaS distinction in the AI-native concept page. Direct read for clearPMM voice: don't sell AGI fantasy; sell measurable transformation per workstream.

10. **The constitutional AI thesis: values produce personality, personality is the brand.** "Claude is one of the least sycophantic models because we've put so much effort into actual alignment, not just trying to good-heart user-engagement metrics." The personality (helpful, honest, harmless) is downstream of the values list (UN Declaration of Human Rights, Apple privacy ToS, Anthropic-generated principles). Direct external precedent for Kesava's operator-voice + kill-list + anti-fabrication discipline — the rules are the upstream that produces the voice that produces brand trust.

11. **Two kinds of unemployment: skills mismatch and job elimination.** Both will happen. The transition period is the scary part; the post-singularity world (capitalism unrecognizable) may be fine. Direct read for clearPMM messaging: don't pitch full automation; pitch the bridge. The contracted PMMs of 2026-2028 are the bridge labor, not the post-singularity workforce. That's the honest framing.

## Other notable threads

- **Anthropic's pace: from "no results on safety" to working safety techniques in 4 years.** "We had tried a bunch of safety through debate and the models weren't good enough... now that exact technique is working." Useful for Kesava's flywheel-build runway argument — the path from "no results" to "working" is shorter than skeptics assume.
- **Sycophancy is the default failure mode of engagement-optimized models.** Anti-sycophancy is the proxy for actual alignment. Cross-link to Jacob Warwick's "say the thing you cannot say" — same shape, different layer. Operators and AI systems both have the failure mode of telling people what they want to hear.
- **Curiosity, kindness, creativity as the durable child-rearing skills for the AI era.** Mann's daughters at Montessori. "I don't think any of it's going to matter. I just want her to be happy and thoughtful and curious and kind." Direct alignment with Kesava's wiki+commons working norms — curiosity is enforced (Curiosity tool, ingest-everything rule); kindness is the captain-rotation + no-drama frame; creativity is the substrate's job.
- **People are bad at modeling exponentials.** "If you look at an exponential on a graph, it looks flat and almost zero at the beginning of it." Cross-link to Peter Deng's portfolio approach — the right time allocation depends on which part of the curve you're on. For Kesava's exit clock: the next 12 weeks are knee-of-the-curve weeks for clearPMM, not flat-of-the-curve weeks.
- **Capitalism in 20 years is hard to imagine.** Mann's honest acknowledgment that singularity = post-forecast horizon. Useful frame for clearPMM long-term — don't over-plan beyond 3-year horizon; structure for optionality (Elena Verna echo).
- **Fewer than 1,000 people worldwide work on alignment.** Vs. $300B/yr industry CapEx. Massive structural mismatch. Useful operator framing — the work that doesn't have 100,000 people on it is where Kesava-class operators have leverage. Same shape as PMM substrate work today.

## Why this matters for flywheel

Three direct loads. First, the Economic Turing Test gives the CXO demo a clean, measurable benchmark. Don't argue "is the substrate good enough?" Show: this LP variant, this vertical playbook, this sales cadence — would JustCall hire a contractor at $X to produce this? If yes, the substrate has passed the test for that workstream. Roll across workstreams; track the money-weighted percentage. That's the revenue-per-FTE arithmetic in a defensible form.

Second, the constitutional-AI-as-personality argument is the strongest external precedent for Kesava's operator-voice discipline. The kill-list, the evidence ladder, the anti-fabrication rule, the no-em-dash rule — these are the constitution. The voice (Kesava's, the team's, the artifacts') is downstream of the constitution. The brand trust is downstream of the voice. When defending the discipline against "isn't this just style?" cite Mann: the constitution is what makes Claude less sycophantic, and that's why customers trust it. Same shape applies to JustCall content + clearPMM voice.

Third, the "use new tools as new tools" + "retry from scratch" patterns are the operating discipline for the substrate-fluent teammates Kesava is selecting for. Most of the team is using LLMs as fancy autocomplete. The shift to ambitious prompting + structural retry is the unlock. Make this an explicit team norm, with examples drawn from Mann's framing. Not "use AI more"; "use AI ambitiously, and retry the whole prompt when it fails."
