# Asha Sharma — Product as organism

## Why this is in the wiki

Asha, CVP at Microsoft AI, sees the shift: products are no longer static artifacts shipped once; they're living organisms that learn from every interaction. Post-training (fine-tuning on proprietary data) is the new pre-training (base model). Reward design, A/B testing loops, job-to-be-done discovery become continuous. "The org chart starts to become the work chart"—fewer layers because agents handle routine work. Directly applicable to Flywheel: GTM automation as living system, not campaign-and-done; continuous learning on messaging, offer, funnel.

## Load-bearing insights for flywheel

1. **Product metabolism: ingest data → reward model → outcome tuning.** "You want to start to tune them to certain types of outcomes... These are living organisms that just get better with the more interactions that happen." No shipped version is final. Every user interaction is training signal. Flywheel lesson: don't treat marketing playbooks as locked docs; tune them on conversion, engagement, NPS signals.

2. **Post-training (not pre-training) is the moat.** Nathan Lambert study: 30B+ parameter models can't justify CapEx pre-training; tuning on proprietary data is better ROI. Cursor's 300M ARR moat: data from accepted/rejected suggestions. Flywheel: proprietary GTM outcomes (which messaging converts, which tactics stick, which verticals respond) become the real asset.

3. **Multiple assembly lines in parallel.** "I haven't ever seen it be one loop for any product. I think it's multiple tracks running in parallel... like assembly lines." Not sequential (collect data → test → ship), but parallel: synthetic data generation, rewards design, A/B testing, job discovery all happening concurrently. Flywheel cadence: weekly ship + continuous tuning.

4. **"What season are we in?" beats long roadmaps.** "We think about it as what season are we in? Season one might've been prototyping of AI and then it was all around models and reasoning models, and now it's the advent of agents." Flywheel is Season 1 (AI-native marketing fundamentals). Seasons shift faster than annual roadmaps; tune the frame.

5. **All software will be model-forward.** "Software products will all be model forward products... the artifact inside of it is a model alongside the software components itself." For Flywheel: automation, copy generation, funnel optimization, offer testing—all model-native, not bolted on.

## Other notable threads

- Org structure collapses: "The org chart starts to become the work chart. You just don't need as many layers."
- Personalization at runtime: "Your experience should look and feel different than mine... you can do it on the fly in the future."
- Success patterns: companies that embrace AI (everyone AI fluent) + trusted rollout (Frontier for early adopters) win.

## Why this matters for flywheel

1. **GTM is no longer campaign-based, it's organism-based.** Launch > observe > tune > observe > iterate. Not "we planned Q2, execute, report." Shipping cadence + feedback loops = the work.

2. **Proprietary GTM data is the moat.** Messaging that works for SaaS vs. verticals vs. personas; which SMSs get responses; which battle card closes deals. This proprietary knowledge (earned from thousands of tests) is worth more than any playbook.

3. **Fewer humans on routine work, more on taste.** As agents/automation handle routine GTM (email sequences, content scheduling, offer testing), the team focuses on taste (which bets matter, which narratives to push, which personas to own).
