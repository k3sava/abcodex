## 12 load-bearing insights for flywheel

1. **The "use case epiphany" was OpenAI's actual marketing job.** Awareness was solved at ChatGPT launch — everyone knew of it. But "I don't know what to use it for" was the bottleneck. Marketing's job became creating moments where users went "I had no idea ChatGPT could do that." This is *exactly* JustCall's problem with the AI Agent + AI Suite product line. Awareness is solved; use-case-epiphany isn't. Flywheel should be measured on "did we create a use-case epiphany this week?" not "did we ship copy?"

2. **DATE diagnostic framework (anti-playbook).** When founders/CEOs ask for the playbook, refuse. Instead: (D) **Diagnose** the actual problem — look at funnel state, not the request; (A) **Analyze** competitor approaches as a baseline + gap-finder; (T) **Take a different path** — differentiation is not optional; (E) **Experiment, test, validate, scale.** Replaces "we need a demand-gen hire" with "do you have a leaky bottom or a leaky top?" Direct flywheel artifact: every Sourav/Reshma intake should run DATE before choosing a tactic.

3. **"Don't litigate vanity metrics — litigate pipeline + revenue."** Retool diagnosis: paid social was driving clicks but not pipeline. They killed it and doubled down on customer storytelling — because the real differentiator was their enterprise customer logos, which competitors couldn't replicate. Marketing analog at JustCall: don't double down on what *seems* like top-of-funnel motion (LinkedIn impressions, blog views). Audit which channel is shipping pipeline-quality leads.

4. **Cheaper is a race to the bottom, especially in AI.** "As models get cheaper, being cheaper is not a durable strategy." Differentiate on novelty, values-fit, problem-space alignment — not price.

5. **Sales rotation as a marketer's discovery loop.** Stripe required all new hires to do support rotation — built customer empathy + revealed product-knowledge gaps. Krithika's first major win was building a stack-ranked backlog of landing pages from support themes ("People keep asking about subscription payments — we never tell them we do it"). Flywheel parallel: weekly support-Slack sweep, mine themes, ship landing pages off them. This is the JustCall help-center-as-marketing thesis Kesava already has.

6. **Brand = velocity multiplier, not velocity tax.** "Companies that value velocity actually value brand just as much — but treat them as siloed. They're interconnected. Brand consistency *empowers* speed because the org understands how to show up." Brand = product experience + support tone + recruiting tone + every touch. Counter to the "move fast, break things, brand can wait" startup default. Flywheel implication: brand-consistency rules in the substrate accelerate teammate ship velocity.

7. **The 20%/80% review checkpoints.** Don't review at 99% (rubber-stamp = useless) or 100% pre-launch only. Review at 20% (strategy: who/why/rough approach) and 80% (artifact ready, still room to change). Skip the 50% review. Flywheel: bake 20%/80% checkpoints into Commons workflow on copy + LP work.

8. **Marketing review forum as fishbowl.** A weekly meeting OR Slack channel OR email alias open to product/eng/marketing. Why: "Osmosis. People learn through watching others get reviewed — strategy framing, audience choice, design approach, copy choice." Flywheel implication: make Commons rooms for review threads visible to the whole org, not closed PMM-internal.

9. **Process empowers new hires.** "If your second-week hire can be as productive as your two-year veteran, you've designed process well." Veterans have "social capital" they can withdraw from to get reviews — that doesn't scale. Process > tribal knowledge for a hyper-growth org. Implication for JustCall: as the team grows + new hires onboard, the substrate's process-as-code is the *only* way to keep them productive on day 14.

10. **Capital-M vs lowercase-m marketing.** Capital-M = the marketing function (channels, artifacts, funnel engines). Lowercase-m = "what does the company stand for? What's the founder's storyline? What's the ICP the sales team is hunting?" — a whole-company motion. Most people conflate them. Flywheel positioning: Kesava's flywheel is *lowercase-m* infrastructure (substrate everyone uses). Sourav/Reshma run the capital-M function. Both required.

11. **Comb-shape > T-shape for modern marketers.** T-shape (deep in one pillar — brand OR demand-gen OR product marketing) is dead. Modern PMM needs analytical-comb (best-friends-with-data-science), creative-comb, technical-comb, brand-comb. AI tools collapse the cost of being multi-domain — analytical brand marketers, creative growth marketers — both are now feasible. Flywheel hiring frame: every PMM teammate must be comb-shaped or be willing to use the substrate to *act* comb-shaped.

12. **Taste = differentiator in the AI era.** "There's going to be so much drivel generated by AI. Companies that distinguish themselves show their craft, their understanding of product + customer, and connect the two." AI-augmented effort, not AI-replaced effort. Build taste via **exposure hours** (Vercel CMO's framing) — share-channels with examples that resonated. Flywheel: a JustCall taste-leaderboard substrate that tracks exposure hours per teammate.

## Other notable threads

- **Stripe Connect zigged via reverse-RFP.** Competitors were "become-a-payfac consulting firms." Stripe ranked for the same SEO terms by writing "If you want to be a payfac, here's the secret playbook — and by the way, this is onerous, just use Stripe Connect." Differentiation pattern: meet competitors on their terms, then redirect.
- **OpenAI cold-start activation lever.** Ask "who-are-you / what-are-you-here-for" questions even though it adds friction. Recommends right product + features afterwards. Friction-as-feature for AI products.
- **Stripe Connect launch saw 40x lead volume overnight.** Krithika built a Python lead-scoring script with ChatGPT — used in production "longer than I'd care to admit." Solo PMM as forced AI-native operator.
- **Work-life blend (Claire Hughes Johnson, ex-Stripe COO):** people + product + potential. Three components for choosing where to work. Not work-life balance — blend.
- **Pricing for AI products = experiment.** No playbook. Retool example: opening up self-hosted to non-enterprise (previously sales-gated) blew up the funnel + freed sales for higher ACV deals. The trade-off was wrenching. AI pricing especially is wild-west — seat-based, usage-based, hours-saved-based, agent-completion-based — no canonical answer yet.
- **Recommended book:** April Dunford's *Obviously Awesome* on positioning. (Already in Kesava's stack via clearPMM.)
- **Life motto:** "The delta between expectations and reality is the function for unhappiness. It's easier to change expectations than reality." → Set expectations relentlessly, internally + externally.

## Why this matters for flywheel

Three specific moves:

1. **DATE as the intake protocol for every flywheel work request.** When a teammate posts to Commons asking for help on a landing page or campaign, run DATE before choosing a tactic. The substrate should walk them through the four steps, not jump to execution. This is the anti-playbook playbook applied at the workflow level.

2. **The "use case epiphany" metric replaces vanity output metrics.** JustCall's AI suite has the same problem ChatGPT had — awareness solved, use-cases not. Flywheel should track per-week: how many use-case epiphanies did we ship across LPs, sales decks, help docs? That's the unit of value.

3. **Comb-shape hiring + taste-leaderboard substrate.** The flywheel substrate's job is to make every teammate comb-shaped — give a creative brand-marketer access to data tooling that an analyst would have, give a growth-marketer access to copywriting craft that a brand-marketer would have. Plus a taste-leaderboard tracking exposure hours per teammate (per Krithika's Vercel quote). This is a concrete substrate feature, not abstract pitch language.
