# Bangaly Kaba — Growth PM philosophy: understand work

## Load-bearing insights for flywheel

1. **Understand work precedes execution.** "First you have to really understand from first principles what is actually going on... understand, identify, execute." Not "pull data to justify a plan" (identify, justify, execute), but "what's really happening?" Data informs understanding, not the other way around. Flywheel: don't start with "we should build AI-native marketing." Start with "what are the biggest PMM bottlenecks in the market?"

2. **Impact = environment + skills.** "Impact is only achievable by looking at two sets of variables... related to the environment... related to your skills." Where are you structurally hindered? Where are your skills lacking? What do you control? Flywheel: which GTM lever (messaging, channel, offer, timing) gives us the highest payoff given our current skills + JustCall's environment?

3. **Adjacent user is the growth inflection point.** Move the target user one notch further (marginal user). They're easier to acquire than greenfield. Flywheel: which vertical/persona is one step adjacent to what we're killing today? Which messaging resonates for them with minimal pivot?

4. **Change framework: vision + skills + incentives + resources + action plan.** All five required. "You need all of those to have change." Flywheel: to scale from 6 to 12 people, every one of the five must align. Missing incentives kills even great ideas.

## Why this matters

Bangaly's philosophy: understand-first, not plan-first. Flywheel is season 1 (understand what AI-native marketing looks like). Don't lock the roadmap; lock the understanding, iterate the execution.
