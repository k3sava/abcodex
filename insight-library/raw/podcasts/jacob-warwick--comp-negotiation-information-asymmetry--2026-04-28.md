# Jacob Warwick — fingerprintless negotiation

## Summary
Jacob negotiates behind the scenes for senior tech execs, professional athletes and Hollywood writers. Over $1B in additional comp delivered. The process (discovery, label the pain, sell the vacation, plant champions, make it frictionless) is the same shape as enterprise B2B sales.

## 11 load-bearing insights

1. **A simple "what's the chance there could be a little more?" yields ~20% lift, almost universally.** "Almost always see a 20% improvement on that, and that is across the board... when you make $1 million W2, same pushback, 20%." With deeper work the average is 40%, with outliers at 100-400%. The cheapest unlock in any negotiation is the soft pushback, and most operators skip it.

2. **Negotiation starts before the first call.** "They don't understand when it starts." LinkedIn snapshot, headshot quality, recruiter call notes, public narrative. "If you are positioned as a commodity, you will be treated like a commodity."

3. **Information asymmetry is the structural problem; you are the werewolf with no card.** "Meta negotiates thousands of times a day. You negotiate four or five times in your career." Werewolf game: even outnumbered 6-to-1, the players with information win 60-70%. Pre-negotiation research and inside coaching aren't optional, they're the entire game.

4. **Never anchor with a number when you can avoid it. Never split the difference reflexively.** Hollywood example: writer at $700K, attorneys ask for $1.3M, studio offers $1M, deal closes at $1M. "If the attorney said $1.5, would the deal have come in at $1.1? That was lazy negotiating." Procedural rule: when terms are wrong, ask "was that a mistake?" before counter-offering.

5. **The discovery-first interview is a consultative sale.** "Some of the best interviews I've always had felt a little bit more like consultations or brainstorming sessions. Are you open to having a chat like that today?" Then: why am I here / what's exciting / it sounds like (label) / what have you done about it / what would success look like in six months.

6. **Sell the vacation, not the credentials.** "Fast-forward six months from now and we've solved the churn... when you head into that board meeting next, how can we ensure your head is held high?" "I've removed the friction and the pain from their shoulders and I forced them to visualize a utopian situation, and who was right next to them doing that? I was."

7. **Plant the champions before the offer stage.** Steven Spielberg story: agency wins the director by booking the lunch with Spielberg before the contract is signed. "If you already had the job, who would you introduce him to?" In a software interview: "I suspect you want me to talk to the head of engineering, the VP marketing, the VP sales. What would you like me to say to them?" You're now stickier in the org and the comp ask gets easier because every VP coached you.

8. **Performance-based triggers expand the pie when base comp is capped.** Tom Brady contract example, plus the chief revenue officer story: when the base/bonus is fixed, push for milestone tranches, equity refresh on ARR thresholds, severance protections. "Some of the ways we're breaking compensation isn't necessarily a busted base comp level... we're putting performance incentives, milestone triggers."

9. **Power = the ability to say no. Practice negotiating even when you don't need the role.** "If you have the ability to say no, you have infinite power." Practical move: continue interview processes even when the comp band is below your number, just for reps. "Just because having that practice is going to make you hundreds of thousands, if not millions more in your career."

10. **Reframe everything in the language the other side already speaks.** "If you are working with a sales leader, a job search process IS an enterprise sales process. If you're in marketing, it's positioning and market research. If you're in product, you're orchestrating a PRD."

11. **Slow the process. Haste equals risk.** "We want to go slower... it shows a little scarcity and thought process to your time, and we get to calculate and collect information." Concrete move: take 2-3 days before responding to an offer; control the time-of-day for the call ("I'm strongest 10am-1pm"); when stuck, ask "if you were in my shoes, what would you ask for?"

## Other notable threads

- **Communication channel matters more than content.** Email loses tone, recruiter loses tone (telephone game). Always escalate to video or in-person. Walking-side-by-side beats face-across-the-table because it reframes the relationship from confrontation to collaboration.
- **Reciprocity-imbalance plant.** Mid-call, make 2-3 introductions to the prospective client before any contract talk. They feel the imbalance, and you also gain a 360-view via three new touchpoints.
- **Positive-reputation framing as a behavioral hack.** "I want to thank you for being an advocate for me and for respecting that I won't share comp figures right now." Locks the recruiter into a role they then have to live up to.
- **Make it about "we", not "me".** CMO severance story: turned an individual ask into a company-wide policy proposal that protected the CEO too. The CEO became the hero; the CMO got the protection.
- **Audacity to raise the precedent.** Companies hide behind "this is what we always do." "Have the audacity to raise the precedent because the next people in line will do that."
- **Don't over-promise.** "Under promise, over deliver. Always." Sell the vacation, then deliver above it. The reputation bruise from over-promising lasts longer than any comp gain.
- **Authenticity beats the persona.** The CMO who got an offer rescinded for "negotiating like a white man" got it back by calling the CEO and saying "I hired a coach, the advice didn't fit me, I tried it anyway." Honesty repaired the cultural-fit signal.
