# Mihika Kapoor — zero-to-one inside a big company

## Summary
Mihika is the Figma PM most-mentioned when Lenny asked Twitter "who's the best PM you've worked with?" She ships zero-to-one products inside a 1000+ person company by combining design-engineer-PM hybrid skills with a particular operating style: strong opinions communicated explicitly with confidence labels, hype manufactured via demos at every internal forum, and a "your scope is the world" stance.

## 11 load-bearing insights

1. **Your scope is the world. Nothing should ever be perceived as out of bounds.** Operating stance, not job description.

2. **Vision is everything because the product cycle is messy.** "You're going to have extreme highs and extreme lows. You're going to march in a certain direction only to hear from your users that it might just be the wrong direction, and totally pivot." The vision is the anchor that makes setbacks feel like learning.

3. **Vision is built cross-functionally, not handed off linearly.** Research → design → engineering linearly = weak vision. Cross-pollinate from the start. "You have to be fundamentally inseparable from your users and from your team." The Medici Effect.

4. **Vision pitch structure: pain point → solution → proof point. Repeat.** Not "pain points, then solutions, then timeline." Interleave them. Each solution claim is paired with a proof point (testimonial, prototype, demo) sitting on top of the mock. "Words will only get you so far... Figma practices what it preaches in terms of the future being visual communication."

5. **Single shared artifact > parallel deliverables.** Don't have research readout + design crit + product review as separate decks. One artifact the team owns together. "You actually want everyone to feel incredible ownership and incredible passion about this combined deliverable, so that it's a unified team who believes a singular set of insights."

6. **Design and prototype before the project is green-lit.** Counter to the "earn the green light through deck logic, then design" default. Figma's "see to believe and see to feel" cuts faster.

7. **Strong-opinion conviction at A-minus, then refine to A-plus via real reactions.** Quote from Mihika's CPO Yuhki: "When you go into research, you want to go in with something that's at least an A-minus idea." If users react and you learn, you get to A-plus. If you don't lead, you stall at B. "Putting out an idea, even if it's totally wrong, is a much better catalyst for getting to a good solution because people are much more likely to react to an idea than to nothing."

8. **Always label your confidence explicitly.** "I'll say, 'Oh, I think we should do this, but I feel medium confidence on it. If you feel stronger, I defer to you.'" The downside of high-conviction PMs is that the room reads conviction as veto. Confidence labels prevent the suppression.

9. **Directness only works if it's two-way. Ask for feedback first.** "If you have feedback to give someone else, start by asking, 'Hey, do you have feedback for me?'" Two reasons: (a) it makes the channel reciprocal, (b) the other person clears their own pressure first, then receives.

10. **Internal hype is a job. Manufacture demos at every forum.** Sales kickoff, all-hands, Maker Week, conference keynotes — these are the natural amplifiers. Insert a demo of the in-progress product even when "not ready." The demo creates company-wide visibility, surfaces real product feedback, and aligns the team. "What ended up happening is something like that, a demo that wasn't meant to be a demo... does so much in terms of driving that sense of hype."

11. **External hype matches the product's brand.** FigJam is "cheeky, fun, friend-like" → birthday party + Easter eggs. Figma is precision craft for designers → niche-feature releases. Apple is unboxing. "Leaning into a product's brand in order to figure out what is the optimal way to generate hype with your audience and form that connection."

## Other notable threads

- **Hackathons are not just for engineers.** Maker Week pitches built Mihika's current product launch. "Walk around the office asking every single person, 'Will you work on this thing with me?' Eventually, someone says yes, and then you can use that to build momentum."
- **One-on-ones every day for a year with the design partner.** Mihika + her designer Kean had hour-long daily 1:1s. Counter to the "1:1s are weekly meetings" default. The cadence matches the work intensity.
- **Talk to non-users to understand the perception barrier.** "Why aren't you using FigJam?" turned into a feature investment in placeholder onboarding. Don't only ask current customers; ask the close-but-not-yet to understand what's gating adoption.
- **Sales team relationship as the live signal channel.** Recurring cadence with sales feeds priority. Mihika made a Loom video showing her own FigJam usage that became sales evangelism material.
- **Asana emoji to capture insights from Slack.** Lightweight signal-capture. React with Asana emoji, becomes a backlog task, weekly grooming. The lightweight capture is more important than the heavy queue.
- **Moments matter more than averages.** Anniversaries, kickoffs, conference launches are emotional inflection points; treat them as full launches.
