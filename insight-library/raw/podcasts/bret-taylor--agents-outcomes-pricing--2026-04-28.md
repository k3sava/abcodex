# Bret Taylor on Agents and Outcomes-Based Pricing

## Why this is in the wiki
Taylor: ex-Google (Maps), Salesforce co-CEO (w/Benioff), Meta CTO, OpenAI Chair. Core thesis: SaaS market pivots to agents + outcomes pricing. Not aspirational; inevitable.

## Insights
1. **Agent-based SaaS is structural inevitable** — Not "agents are cool." The market moves there because outcomes pricing is obviously correct. Pay for results, not seats. Changes everything about selling/building.

2. **Productivity software is hard to sell (legacy)** — Benioff & Taylor both "extremely agent-pilled" at Salesforce. Friction in adoption, slow ROI, adoption resistance. Agents remove friction by owning outcomes.

3. **Failure → reset → success** — Google Local flopped (harsh product review). Gave V2 shot → Google Maps (10M users day 1). Institutional forgiveness + execution excellence = scale. Learn fast, don't dwell.

4. **Morning framing: "Most impactful thing today?"** — Not productivity theater. Ruthless impact focus. Compounds across years into career optionality (Google, Salesforce, Meta, OpenAI).

5. **Outcome-based pricing changes incentives** — No longer "push more licenses." Aligned: you win when customer succeeds. Model shifts profit to providers who can actually deliver.

## Why this matters for flywheel
Agents eating SaaS from bottom-up. Flywheel's GTM agents move JustCall from "software" to "outcomes marketplace." Pricing model shift unlocks LTV.
