# Hype Cycle for Agentic AI

120-min flagship Hype Cycle by Rajesh Kandaswamy et al. introducing agentic AI as a distinct innovation track. Thesis: agentic AI is a fundamentally new software paradigm but supporting infrastructure (integration, reliability, security, governance, FinOps) is still maturing. The market is shaped by extreme hype, "agent-washing" by legacy RPA/automation vendors, and an aggressive enterprise adoption curve.

For flywheel positioning, the load-bearing claim is that current LLM-based agents are varied, semi-autonomous at best, and that fully-autonomous agents are not ready for most enterprise use cases. Human oversight is mandatory. This is the antidote to the "AI replaces humans" narrative — it's also the through-line that justifies the flywheel's "humans do highest-value jobs" frame.

## Key takeaways

- 17% of orgs have deployed AI agents; 42% expect to in next 12 months; 22% within the year after — most aggressive adoption curve among emerging tech in Gartner's 2026 CIO survey.
- Most enterprise use is automating existing workflows, not reengineering for agentic AI. Software engineering is the exception (real reengineering happening with agentic coding).
- Agent-washing: legacy automation/RPA rebranded as agent platforms — buyer confusion is a marketing surface.
- Five innovation areas: Agent Development, Agent Integration & Deployment, Agent Human Interaction, Agent Trust & Risk, Agent Foundation Models.
- Emerging primitives: agent communication protocols (MCP), multiagent orchestration, agentic browsers, human-agent collaboration workspaces, agent marketplaces, world models, context graphs.
- AI agents ≠ LLMs. Agents can use deterministic models too — and for reliability + transparency, often should.
- Semi-autonomous (not fully autonomous) is what enterprises must plan for.

## Quotes worth keeping

> "Hype and interest in AI agents have reached extraordinary levels, making it vital for CIOs and AI leaders to gain clarity on what's real, what's emerging, and what's simply noise." (p.2)

> "Only 17% of organizations have deployed AI agents so far, but 42% expect to do so in the next 12 months, and another 22% within the following year. This is the most aggressive adoption curve among all emerging technologies in the survey." (p.2)

> "In practice, fully autonomous agents are not ready for most enterprise use cases, and human oversight remains essential. Semiautonomous deployments, where there is some human supervision of the work of AI agents, are what enterprises must plan for." (p.2)

> "Agent-washing, where legacy automation tools and RPA solutions have been rebranded as AI agent building platforms... muddies the market." (p.3)

> "These innovations are shifting the focus from code to capability, and from deterministic logic to adaptive behavior." (p.4)
