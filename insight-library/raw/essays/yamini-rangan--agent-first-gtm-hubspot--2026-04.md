# Raw — How we Grow with Agent-first GTM (Yamini Rangan, HubSpot, 2026-04-28)

Part 2 of a 3-part HubSpot series. Part 1 = how we build with AI. Part 3 = how we operate as an AI-first company.

## Headline numbers (verbatim)

- Demand Agent: **+345,000 accounts added to TAM** in the last year (accounts reps would otherwise have lacked sufficient data to pursue).
- AEO Agent: **qualified leads from AI-generated answers grew 1,850%** between Q1 2025 and Q1 2026. **Convert at up to 3x the rate of traditional search.** "HubSpot is now the most visible CRM in LLMs."
- Inbound Agent: **handles 82% of all inbound chats with zero human involvement.** Beginning to sell HubSpot Starter when there is a clear fit.
- Prospecting Agent: **books over 10,000 meetings per quarter** through personalized multi-touch sequences.
- Guided Sales Assistant: **13% increase in win rate** for deals where AI guidance is used.
- Customer Agent: resolves **~60% of internal support inquiries without human intervention.**
- Customer Success Assistant: **80%+ of CSMs use it weekly. 7-point higher customer save rate.**

## The three-stage flywheel

**Attract → Engage → Delight.** Three bets per stage:

### Attract
1. **Demand Agent** — identifies ICP, finds matching companies, enriches contacts with multi-source signals, generates a prospect value score (likelihood to close × expected ARR).
2. **Inbound Agent** — chatbot on website. Qualifies visitors, handles competitive questions, propensity scoring on buying intent, books meetings, closes Starter deals autonomously.
3. **AEO Agent** — makes HubSpot visible and credible in AI-generated responses (ChatGPT, Perplexity).

### Engage
- **Prospecting Agent** — orchestrates across all channels (email alone underperformed; multi-touch + intent signals + rep-task generation works).
- **Guided Sales Assistant** — conversational interface over deal pipeline. Reps ask "what's the risk on this deal," "how did we win in similar situations," "what should I do next" instead of staring at a dashboard.
- **Pre-sales Agent** — handles complex technical questions a specialist would otherwise field.
- **Demo Agent** — spins up tailored demo environments per industry/geo/company size.

### Delight
- **Customer Agent** — internal support deflection (60%).
- **Customer Success Assistant** — answers "which accounts should I focus on today?", drafts outreach, flags engagement opportunities. CSMs report better conversations grounded in context.
- **Digital Success Agent** — for unassigned-CSM customers: product guidance + next-step recommendations + capability surfacing.

## Operating model claim (verbatim, load-bearing)

> "agents are doing real work at every stage and humans are operating with higher impact, connecting more deeply with customers"

> "Instead of replacing human connection, agents have enabled deeper connection."

> "no agent is working alone… that is the flywheel. And it gets stronger with every interaction."

## Specificity assessment

**High-fidelity data:** 345K, 82%, 1,850%, 10K, 13%, 60%, 7-point — all concrete and falsifiable.

**Aspirational/vague:** "real work," "higher impact," "deeper connection," "full context" lack operational definition.

## Notable gap in the article

No discussion of failure modes, agent accuracy bounds, when humans override, or which bets didn't pay off. All outcomes presented as positive. Calibration / Brier-style honesty absent.

## A note on availability (verbatim)

> "Many of the capabilities described in this series are a mix of HubSpot-built tools, third-party agents, and custom integrations. Some are available in HubSpot: AEO features, data enrichment to drive demand, and agents for customer support and prospecting. Assistants like Guided Sales Assistant and our Customer Success Assistant can also be built as custom assistants."

So: HubSpot ships the AEO + demand + support + prospecting layer as product; the Guided Sales / CS Assistant layer is custom-built per their scale.
