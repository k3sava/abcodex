# David Ogilvy — canonical principles

David Mackenzie Ogilvy (1911–1999) was a British advertising executive and
founder of Ogilvy & Mather, widely recognized as the "Father of Advertising."
His methodology emphasized meticulous consumer research, intelligent customer
treatment, and measurable results over creative flourish alone.

## Verbatim quotes (canonical)

### From *Confessions of an Advertising Man* (1963)

1. "The consumer isn't a moron; she is your wife. You insult her intelligence
   if you assume that a mere slogan and a few vapid adjectives will persuade
   her to buy anything." — p. 96

2. "Never Write an Advertisement Which You Wouldn't Want Your Own Family To
   Read." — p. 87

### From *Ogilvy on Advertising* (1983)

3. "When I write an advertisement, I don't want you to tell me that you find
   it 'creative'. I want you to find it so interesting that you _buy the
   product_." — p. 7

4. "If each of us hires people who are bigger than we are, we shall become a
   company of _giants_." — p. 47

5. "There are now unmistakeable signs of a trend in favor of superior products
   at premium prices. The consumer is not a moron, she is your wife." — p. 170

6. "Viewers have a way of remembering the celebrity while forgetting the
   product." — *Ogilvy on Advertising*

### From Wikipedia bio (general attribution)

7. "I prefer the discipline of knowledge to the anarchy of ignorance." —
   attributed via Wikipedia, citing Ogilvy's published works
