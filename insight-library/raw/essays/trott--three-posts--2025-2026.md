# Dave Trott — three blog posts

Dave Trott (b. 1947) is a British creative director and copywriter. Trained
at Pratt Institute (NY) and Carl Ally Inc. before returning to London in
1971. Co-founded Gold Greenlees Trott (GGT, 1980, voted Most Creative Agency
in the World by Advertising Age), Bainsfair Sharkey Trott (1990), Walsh
Trott Chick Smith / The Gate (Chairman until 2014). D&AD President's Award
2004.

Books: *Creative Mischief* (2010), *Predatory Thinking: A Masterclass in
Out-Thinking the Competition* (2013), *One + One = Three* (2016), *Crossover
Creativity*.

## Verbatim quotes

### "DON'T PLAY THE GAME, CHANGE THE GAME" (April 27, 2026)

> "the alternative to efficiency wasn't more efficiency, the alternative was
> a different idea."

> "And it is unbeatable if you play it at its own game. Which is why you need
> to change the game to a game where you can beat it."

> "AI isn't magic; as Steve Jobs says, it's dumb but it's fast."

### "POINT-OF-SAMENESS" (January 20, 2025)

> "If no advertising has a point-of-difference, all advertising has is a
> point-of-sameness."

> "The internet is Tokyo. The internet allows you to be niche at scale."

> "We used to talk about what made our product different, why anyone should
> choose to buy our product instead of a competitor."

> "That's why every commercial says the same thing: rather than tell you why
> our product is different, and risk being seen as a specialist, we tell you
> our product will simply make you happy and we symbolise that happiness
> with dancing."

### "WHAT NOEL GALLAGHER CAN TEACH US" (January 13, 2025)

> "How you get a campaign to go viral is to trigger your audience to repeat
> it until it takes on a life of its own and becomes what's called a meme."

> "Using our initial campaign as seed-corn to create advertising we're not
> paying for."

> "Getting our ideas to go viral means 'word-of-mouth' and that's the most
> valuable advertising space of all."
