# Excerpt (from miniu digest 2026-04-27, 2026-04-28)

Kevin Indig's research across 3,981 domains and four AI engines (ChatGPT, Gemini, Google AI, one other), 454 prompt-domain pairs:

- 62% of citations across the sample never name the brand.
- Comparative content gets brands named; informational content feeds the machine anonymously.
- Certain query formats and content types produced 30x more brand mentions in the same sample.

Load-bearing distinction:
> "Being cited means an AI is drawing on your content. Being mentioned means it is naming you."

Follow-up Growth Memo Brief #17 (https://www.growth-memo.com/p/growth-intelligence-brief-17): 65 to 85% of what people ask ChatGPT is invisible to traditional keyword tools. Through-line:
> "AI search and traditional search are becoming complementary surfaces, not substitutes."
