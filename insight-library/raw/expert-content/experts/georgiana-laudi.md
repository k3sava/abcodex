Georgiana Laudi is the architect of Customer-Led Growth (CLG) as a strategic framework, and her most distinctive contribution is the Customer Experience Map -- a methodology that reframes the entire customer journey around success milestones rather than business-centric funnel stages. Her intellectual provenance comes from in-house marketing leadership at Unbounce, where she recognized that traditional funnel-based marketing (awareness, consideration, decision) optimizes for the company's transaction moments rather than the customer's success moments. This insight, combined with the Airbnb experience mapping approach that inspired her early work, led to a fundamentally different way of structuring GTM strategy.

The Customer Experience Map divides the customer journey into three phases, but critically, these phases are defined by the customer's state of mind, not the company's pipeline stage. The Struggle phase encompasses everything happening in the customer's world before they find your product -- the pain, the workaround, the trigger that pushes them to search. The Evaluation phase covers the period when customers are assessing whether your product fits their job-to-be-done. The Growth phase represents the post-adoption period where customers have embedded the product into recurring habits and the opportunity shifts to expansion, deeper usage, and advocacy. Within each phase, specific success milestones are identified based on customer research -- these milestones become the KPIs that matter, replacing traditional vanity metrics (MQLs, SQLs) with customer-outcome metrics (time to first value, feature adoption breadth, expansion triggers).

Laudi's CLG framework is explicitly positioned as a meta-framework that transcends the PLG/SLG/MLG debate. Her argument is that whether you go to market through product-led, sales-led, or marketing-led motions, the underlying success depends on understanding what customers need at each milestone. PLG companies that optimize onboarding without understanding the customer's job still churn; SLG companies that close deals without mapping the post-sale success journey lose expansion revenue. The CLG process has a specific operational workflow: (1) identify best-fit customers using behavioral and revenue criteria, (2) conduct JTBD research (co-developed with Suellentrop) to unpack the switch journey, (3) build the Customer Experience Map with success milestones, (4) audit every customer-facing touchpoint against those milestones, (5) prioritize the biggest gaps between current experience and ideal experience, (6) build, measure, iterate. When multiple customer segments emerge, Laudi recommends prioritizing the one segment that would move the revenue needle most in the next 6-9 months, serving other segments with a generic experience temporarily. This connects to Grominsky's segment-first GTM (segmentation drives execution priority), to Verna's PLG (product experience must map to customer milestones), and to Shah's PMF methodology (the Customer Experience Map is essentially a post-PMF operationalization tool).
