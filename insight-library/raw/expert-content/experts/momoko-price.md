Momoko Price occupies the intersection of data-driven optimization and voice-of-customer authenticity that most copywriters leave empty. Her messaging-first optimization methodology starts not with creative brainstorming but with systematic customer language research. The copy that converts best is the copy that sounds like the customer already wrote it.

Her VoC research process is more rigorous than typical review mining. She conducts structured customer interviews, mines review sites with specific search queries, analyzes support tickets for recurring language patterns, and builds a linguistic database of exact phrases customers use to describe their problems, desires, and objections. The copy is then assembled from these authentic fragments, not invented from scratch.

The customer-language-first positioning method: instead of writing a positioning statement and then translating it into customer language, Price works in reverse. She collects customer language first, identifies the recurring emotional themes, and builds the positioning from those themes upward. The result is positioning that sounds native to the audience because it literally came from them.

Her analytics integration distinguishes her from pure-craft copywriters. Every messaging hypothesis is testable. She designs copy variations that isolate specific variables (headline framing, proof placement, CTA language) and runs structured A/B tests to validate which customer language patterns actually convert. The winning copy is not the most creative. It is the most resonant, as measured by behavior.

Applied to landing pages: Price's method produces pages where the hero headline is a customer quote (lightly edited), the problem section uses the exact language from support tickets, and the feature descriptions mirror how customers actually talk about the product (not how the product team describes it). The page feels like the reader wrote it because, in a meaningful sense, they did.
