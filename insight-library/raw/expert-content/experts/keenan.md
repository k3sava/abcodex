Keenan is the most philosophically radical of the modern sales methodologists. Where others provide frameworks that can be layered onto existing selling approaches, Keenan argues that selling must be rebuilt from first principles. His nine truths function as axioms from which all selling behavior should derive. The most foundational: "No problem, no sale." The most provocative: "No one gives a shit about you or your product." These are not motivational slogans; they are operational principles that dictate how every outreach, discovery call, presentation, and proposal should be structured, always starting from the buyer's problems and never from the seller's product.

The Gap Selling methodology is built on the concept of the gap between current state and future state. The current state encompasses the buyer's environment (tools, processes, team structure), problems (what is not working), impact (business and personal consequences), and root causes (why the problems exist). The future state encompasses desired outcomes (measurable business results), emotional outcomes (how the buyer wants to feel), and solution requirements (capabilities needed). The gap is the distance between these two states, and it is the only thing that creates buying motivation. Keenan's Change Formula formalizes this: Change = (Dissatisfaction with Current State x Desirability of Future State) > Cost of Change. If either dissatisfaction or desirability is zero, the product is zero, and no change happens regardless of price or competitive positioning.

The methodology places discovery at the center of the sales process, arguing it should consume the first 25% of the total sales cycle. Keenan teaches four question types: Probing (understand current state), Provoking (make the buyer think differently about their situation), Validating (confirm understanding and build agreement), and Process (map decision-making dynamics). The Problem Identification Chart is a pre-call preparation tool that maps every problem your product solves, its business impact, and its root cause, serving simultaneously as outreach preparation, discovery navigation, and qualification criteria. His "never sell to need" principle argues that solving the buyer's surface-level stated need, rather than the root cause discovered through deep investigation, produces smaller deals, less satisfied customers, and higher churn. Gap Selling is intellectually complementary to SPIN Selling (both are discovery-centric) but approaches from a different philosophical angle: SPIN is questioning-technique-centric (how to ask), while Gap Selling is problem-architecture-centric (what to explore). The gap concept also directly addresses the "no decision" problem that Matt Dixon's JOLT Effect targets: a sufficiently large, well-quantified gap creates enough motivation to overcome both status quo bias and indecision.
