Marie Haynes is the SEO industry's leading authority on Google algorithm recovery, having built her entire practice around helping sites that have been penalized by Google core updates, Panda, Penguin, and manual actions regain their rankings. Her diagnostic methodology begins with Google's Quality Rater Guidelines (QRG), the 170+ page document that Google uses to train its 12,000+ human quality raters. Haynes was one of the first SEO practitioners to recognize that the QRG is not just a training document for raters but a roadmap for what Google's algorithms are being trained to evaluate. Her audit process systematically compares a site's E-E-A-T signals against the criteria in the QRG, identifying specific gaps in expertise demonstration, authoritativeness signals, and trust indicators.

Her algorithm recovery workflow follows a structured diagnostic process. First, timing analysis: correlate the traffic decline with specific known Google updates to identify which ranking system likely caused the drop (core update, HCU, spam update, link spam update). Second, QRG-based audit: evaluate the site against the quality rater guidelines, specifically examining whether the site demonstrates E-E-A-T compared to competitors ranking for the same queries. Common deficiencies she identifies include lack of named authors with verifiable credentials, thin "me too" content that adds no unique value, YMYL content (health, finance, legal) without demonstrated professional expertise, poor site reputation signals (negative reviews, BBB complaints, lack of industry citations), and affiliate-heavy content that prioritizes monetization over user value. Third, competitive gap analysis: compare the penalized site's E-E-A-T signals against the sites that now rank in the positions it lost. Fourth, remediation plan: specific actions to close the quality gap, typically including content pruning (removing or consolidating thin pages), author attribution improvements, expertise demonstration (credentials, certifications, bylines), trust signal building (reviews, testimonials, industry citations), and technical cleanup of any spam signals.

Haynes's cross-disciplinary contribution is demonstrating that Google's quality evaluation system is converging with principles that brand strategists and positioning experts already understand: trust, credibility, and demonstrated expertise are not just marketing concepts but algorithmic ranking factors. Her work shows that the distinction between "SEO" and "building a genuinely trustworthy brand" is narrowing with each algorithm update. For B2B SaaS companies, her framework implies that investing in thought leadership, named expert authors, original research, and genuine customer evidence is not just good marketing but structurally necessary for maintaining organic visibility. Sites that treat content as a commodity to be produced at scale, without genuine expertise behind it, face increasing algorithmic risk.
