Sam Oh has become the most authoritative voice in video SEO through his dual role as both a practitioner (growing the Ahrefs YouTube channel to 500K+ subscribers) and an educator (creating Ahrefs' most comprehensive guides on video optimization). His methodology distinguishes between two fundamentally different video SEO disciplines: ranking videos within YouTube's own search and discovery system, and ranking videos in Google's search results. These require different optimization strategies because they rely on different ranking signals and serve different user intents.

For YouTube-native SEO, Oh's framework prioritizes audience retention and watch time as the dominant ranking factors, with keyword optimization as a necessary but insufficient condition. His process follows a specific sequence: (1) keyword research using YouTube-specific tools and YouTube's autocomplete suggestions to find queries with search demand within the platform, (2) search intent analysis by reviewing the top-ranking videos for a target keyword to understand what format, length, and angle YouTube rewards, (3) scripting and production optimized for retention, including hooking viewers in the first 15 seconds, using pattern interrupts to maintain attention, and structuring content to deliver on the title's promise without unnecessary preamble, (4) metadata optimization including titles that balance keyword inclusion with click-worthiness, descriptions that provide context for YouTube's NLP system, and tags as a secondary signal, and (5) thumbnail optimization, which he considers the single highest-leverage element because click-through rate in browse features and suggested videos determines how much distribution YouTube gives a video.

For Google video SEO (ranking YouTube videos in Google's main search results), Oh identifies a different optimization approach. Google surfaces video results primarily for queries where video intent is strong (how-to, reviews, tutorials, entertainment), and the ranking factors blend YouTube engagement signals with traditional web SEO signals including the video's embed context, the hosting page's authority, and structured data markup (VideoObject schema). His key insight is that not all queries have video intent in Google, and targeting queries where Google already shows video carousels or dedicated video results is far more effective than trying to force video results for text-dominant queries. For content strategists and demand-gen leaders, Oh's work demonstrates that YouTube is a full search engine with its own discovery algorithm, not just a video hosting platform, and that video content strategy requires the same keyword research, intent analysis, and competitive analysis rigor as traditional SEO, applied to a platform where engagement metrics rather than backlinks determine visibility.
