Crayon is a competitive intelligence platform that has codified best practices for battlecard creation, CI distribution, and sales enablement through competitive intelligence. Their perspective is shaped by working with hundreds of CI teams and analyzing industry-wide patterns — they report that 68% of sales opportunities are competitive and nearly two-thirds of CI teams produce battlecards. This positions them as a practitioner-aggregate source rather than a single-expert viewpoint.

Their battlecard framework centers on three principles: Accuracy, Brevity, and Consistency (ABC). The content model is seller-informed rather than analyst-driven — you determine what goes in a battlecard by talking to sales leaders about wins and losses, not by compiling comprehensive competitive analysis. Core sections (Why We Win, Competitor Strengths, Recent Wins, Recent Field Intel, Landmines and Quick Dismiss) are designed for in-deal usage, with copy-paste-ready language for seller emails and scannable formats that can be consumed in under a minute.

Where Crayon's perspective is most distinctive is on battlecard adoption. They argue that content quality is necessary but insufficient — the real challenge is distribution and behavior change. Their two-pronged adoption strategy (effortless access in existing workflows plus role model behavior from top performers) draws on McKinsey's research on behavior change. The emphasis on embedding battlecards in CRM at the opportunity level, Slack/Teams channels, and even calendar invitations reflects a design-for-the-workflow philosophy that many CI teams miss when they focus solely on content quality.
