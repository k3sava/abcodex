Eddie Shleyner's approach to copywriting is built on the conviction that emotion is not a nice-to-have layer on top of persuasion mechanics. It IS the persuasion mechanism. His VeryGoodCopy newsletter delivers micro-lessons (2-3 minutes each) on clarity, conciseness, vividness, and emotional truth in writing. Each lesson typically isolates one craft principle, demonstrates it with a before/after example, and explains the psychology behind it.

At G2, Shleyner developed what he calls Transformational Landing Pages, achieving conversion rates above 60% by channeling existing demand rather than trying to manufacture it. His insight: most landing pages fail not because the copy is poorly written but because it is trying to persuade people who do not yet feel anything. The fix is to write from genuine feeling. "Writing while emotional" is not unprofessional. It is a deliberate strategy. The writer's emotional investment transfers to the reader.

His headline philosophy: 80% of your writing time should be spent on the headline, because 80% of readers never get past it. This aligns with Ogilvy's 80/20 rule but Shleyner adds a modern nuance: in the age of infinite scroll, the headline is not just the most-read element. It is often the ONLY element. Every headline must function as a complete persuasive argument in itself.

Key principles: (1) Clarity beats cleverness, always. A headline that requires interpretation is a headline that fails. (2) Vividness creates memorability. Abstract claims disappear from memory; concrete images persist. (3) Conciseness is respect. Every unnecessary word signals that you value your message more than the reader's time. (4) Emotion is the cornerstone. Logic tells people what to think. Emotion tells them what to do.
