Sangram Vajre is the evangelist of account-based go-to-market. His provocative thesis, "ABM is B2B," argues that account-based marketing is not a tactic, a campaign type, or an alternative to demand generation, but the structurally correct way to operate in B2B because the buying unit in B2B is an account (an organization with 6-10 decision-makers), not a lead (an individual). The traditional marketing funnel (attract many leads, qualify some, close few) was designed for B2C volume sales and has been awkwardly retrofitted onto B2B, where it creates structural misalignment: marketing is measured on leads, sales is measured on revenue, and the gap between these metrics creates the endemic sales-marketing conflict that plagues most B2B organizations.

His FlipMyFunnel concept inverts the traditional funnel from Awareness -> Interest -> Consideration -> Purchase into an account-first model: Identify (target the right accounts) -> Expand (reach the full buying committee) -> Engage (create meaningful interactions) -> Advocate (turn customers into champions). This inversion changes the fundamental operating logic from "generate volume and filter down" to "select precisely and build up." The TEAM framework (Target, Engage, Activate, Measure) operationalizes ABM into four stages. Target: identify accounts using firmographic, technographic, and intent data, organized into three tiers (1:1 strategic, 1:few segment, 1:many programmatic). Engage: deploy multi-channel, buying-committee-wide engagement designed to create awareness and preference, not "leads." Activate: hand off engaged accounts to sales with intelligence packages (engagement history, content consumed, stakeholders identified), triggered by engagement score thresholds. Measure: evaluate on business outcomes (pipeline velocity, deal size, win rate, LTV) not marketing activities (MQLs, email opens, cost per lead).

His later MOVE framework extends beyond ABM into a comprehensive GTM operating model built on four questions: Market (whom should we target?), Operations (what infrastructure do we need?), Velocity (when can we scale?), and Expansion (where can we grow most?). These questions are answered differently at three GTM maturity stages: Ideation (problem-market fit, lead-focused, sales-led), Transition (product-market fit, account-focused, aligned teams), and Execution (platform-market fit, customer-focused, integrated revenue team). The MOVE framework's maturity stages parallel Mark Roberge's Science of Scaling (both warn against premature scaling and use stage-appropriate tactics). Vajre's account-based model challenges Aaron Ross's lead-centric Predictable Revenue model: in a flipped funnel, Spears become targeted account outreach, Nets become account-level inbound, and Seeds become customer advocacy. His emphasis on post-sale expansion and customer advocacy connects to Jacco van der Kooij's Bowtie Model, particularly the right side (Onboarding, Impact, Expansion), making Vajre's framework one of the few in the sales methodology space that explicitly addresses where 80%+ of recurring revenue value is actually created.
