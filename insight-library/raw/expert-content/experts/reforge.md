Reforge's approach to growth strategy is distinguished by its insistence on systems thinking over tactics. Their core philosophy treats growth as a set of quantitative models — loops, funnels, and retention curves — that must be understood mechanically before they can be optimized. This contrasts with the common approach of collecting growth "hacks" without understanding why they work or when they stop working.

On product-led growth, Reforge frames PLG as a major growth motion (like sales-led or marketing-led) rather than a bag of tricks. PLG works when the product itself is the primary vehicle for acquisition, activation, and expansion — not when PLG tactics are bolted onto a fundamentally sales-led business. The distinction matters because it determines organizational structure, metrics, and investment priorities. Reforge identifies the land-and-expand pattern as the dominant PLG revenue mechanism for B2B: acquire individual users or small teams at low friction, then expand through usage-based triggers and team-level value demonstration.

On growth models, Reforge emphasizes that North Star Metrics can deceive when they conflate different user behaviors or fail to distinguish between growth drivers. Their framework for growth model design requires decomposing a single top-line metric into its component inputs, identifying which inputs are controllable, and building feedback loops that compound over time. The PQL (Product Qualified Lead) concept — scoring users based on product behavior rather than demographic fit — is a key operational mechanism in their PLG framework, bridging the gap between self-serve adoption and sales-assisted expansion.
