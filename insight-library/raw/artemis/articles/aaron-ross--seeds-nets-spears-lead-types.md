# Seeds, Nets, and Spears: The Three Lead Types

One of Aaron Ross's most enduring contributions to B2B sales methodology is his taxonomy of lead types. Rather than treating all leads as equivalent, Ross categorizes them into three types based on their source, conversion characteristics, and scalability. This taxonomy provides a strategic framework for pipeline planning and resource allocation.

## Seeds: Word-of-Mouth and Organic Leads

Seeds grow organically through customer referrals, word of mouth, community engagement, and reputation.

**Characteristics**:
- Highest conversion rate of all three types (typically 2-5x higher than Spears)
- Longest time to develop (months to years of relationship-building)
- Lowest marginal cost per lead
- Cannot be forced or accelerated through spending
- Directly correlated with customer success and satisfaction

**How to grow Seeds**: Invest in customer success and onboarding, create referral programs, build community, encourage industry participation, publish original research.

**Strategic role**: Long-term compounding investment. Should be a growing percentage of total pipeline over time but cannot be sole source for hypergrowth.

## Nets: Inbound Marketing Leads

Nets are captured through marketing activities that attract potential buyers.

**Characteristics**:
- Medium conversion rate (higher than Spears, lower than Seeds)
- Moderate time to develop (weeks to months)
- Scalable with budget, but subject to diminishing returns
- Quality varies significantly based on channel and targeting

**How to generate Nets**: Content marketing, SEO, paid advertising, events, partner co-marketing.

**Strategic role**: Engine of most B2B marketing organizations. Every market has a ceiling on inbound demand.

## Spears: Outbound Prospecting Leads

Spears are generated through targeted, proactive outreach by SDRs using Cold Calling 2.0.

**Characteristics**:
- Lowest conversion rate of the three types
- Most predictable lead source (output directly proportional to SDR input)
- Fastest to scale (hire more SDRs, generate more pipeline)
- Highest cost per lead (SDR salaries, tools, management)
- Highest degree of targeting control

**Strategic role**: Foundation of predictable revenue because output is directly proportional to input. The only lead type where you can directly control output by controlling input.

## Portfolio Strategy: Balancing the Three Types

Ross argues most companies over-invest in one type and under-invest in others. The optimal strategy is a balanced portfolio:

- **Early stage** (pre-PMF): Focus on Seeds. Founder's network and early customer relationships are most valuable.
- **Growth stage** (PMF to GTM Fit): Add Nets through content marketing. Begin building SDR team for Spears.
- **Scale stage** (GTM Fit achieved): Full portfolio. Seeds grow through customer success. Nets expand through channel diversification. Spears scale through SDR team growth.

No mature B2B company should rely on fewer than two types. Single-source dependency creates existential pipeline risk.

## Connection to Pipeline Math

Each type has different conversion rates and cycle times, so the formula runs separately:

- Seeds pipeline = referral volume x seed conversion rate x average deal size
- Nets pipeline = inbound lead volume x MQL-to-SQL rate x net win rate x average deal size
- Spears pipeline = SDR count x qualified opps per SDR per month x spear win rate x average deal size

Total predictable revenue = Seeds pipeline + Nets pipeline + Spears pipeline

This disaggregated view reveals which pipeline source is underperforming and where to invest next.
