Brian Dean built Backlinko into one of the most authoritative SEO resources on the internet by practicing what he taught: creating content so comprehensively useful that it attracted links naturally while also being systematically promoted through outreach. His signature contribution is the Skyscraper Technique, a three-step link building methodology: (1) find content that already has significant backlinks using tools like Ahrefs, (2) create something substantially better along dimensions of depth, freshness, design, or data, and (3) reach out to sites that linked to the original piece and suggest they link to your superior version instead. His original case study demonstrated a 110% increase in organic traffic from a single post using this approach, with a 17-link yield from 160 outreach emails (approximately 11% success rate).

What distinguishes Dean's thinking from most SEO practitioners is his relentless focus on content quality as the primary ranking variable. His on-page SEO framework treats technical optimization as table stakes and emphasizes that Google is increasingly sophisticated at evaluating whether content actually satisfies user intent. His methodology includes specific tactical patterns: front-loading keywords in title tags and H1s, using short URLs, embedding multimedia to increase dwell time, writing introductions that use the APP method (Agree, Promise, Preview) to hook readers immediately, and structuring content for featured snippet capture. But these tactics sit within a larger strategic framework where the goal is to be genuinely the best resource, not to trick algorithms.

His 2026 evolution piece on "How I Would Launch Backlinko Today" reveals how his thinking has adapted to the AI era. He now emphasizes building a personal brand and community alongside SEO, creating content with original data and unique perspectives that AI cannot easily replicate, and developing multichannel distribution strategies that include YouTube and social media rather than relying solely on Google organic. This represents a convergence with Fishkin's audience-first philosophy: even the person who built his career on Google SEO now acknowledges that diversification away from sole dependence on search traffic is structurally necessary. His link building strategies have evolved from pure outreach to include digital PR, creating linkable assets (original research, free tools, visual content), and building relationships with journalists and bloggers who become recurring link sources.
