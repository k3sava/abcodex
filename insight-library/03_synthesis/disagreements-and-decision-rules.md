# Disagreements and Decision Rules

## Contradiction 1: Positioning stability vs rapid iteration
- View A (Dunford/Raskin): keep strategic spine stable; iterate expression, not core thesis.
- View B (growth operators): iterate fast across messaging based on market response.

### Decision rule
Lock strategic narrative quarterly; allow weekly tactical copy experiments within guardrails.

## Contradiction 2: Brand-led demand vs capture-led demand
- View A (Gerhardt-style): long-term brand and audience trust drives durable demand.
- View B (performance-led): near-term pipeline requires capture optimization and tight attribution.

### Decision rule
Use a split portfolio: 60% compounding brand/distribution, 40% high-intent capture. Rebalance by pipeline health.

## Contradiction 3: Community depth vs scale efficiency
- View A (Rosie Sherry): trust quality precedes growth volume.
- View B (growth pressure): scale quickly to hit top-funnel goals.

### Decision rule
Do not scale acquisition until trust KPIs pass threshold (returning contributor rate, moderation load, response latency).

## Contradiction 4: AI automation vs human taste
- View A: maximize automation for speed.
- View B: preserve expert judgment for narrative and risk.

### Decision rule
Automate draft generation and clustering; keep approval checkpoints for positioning, proof claims, and executive messaging.

## Contradiction 5: Last-touch simplicity vs multi-touch realism
- View A: simple attribution is easier to operationalize.
- View B: multi-touch is truer to real buying journeys.

### Decision rule
Use dual reporting: operational last-touch for weekly execution, cohort multi-touch for monthly strategy decisions.
