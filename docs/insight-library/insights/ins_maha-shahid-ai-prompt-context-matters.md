# AI output quality depends on prompt context, not the tool.

## Claim
Most people treat AI like Google with random words and expect genius, but it's more like asking for directions, you have to say where you're starting from. Adding context about the business, customers, and desired tone transforms vague responses into useful ones.

## Mechanism
Large language models generate responses based on the information provided in the prompt. Without sufficient context, the model defaults to generic patterns, producing repetitive or irrelevant output. By specifying the business, audience, and tone, you constrain the model's output space to relevant, high-quality responses.
